# The Kernel Taxonomy

The kernel is domain-agnostic: one grounding core, the same for every subject, since its job is to be the part that does not depend on what claims are about. Because the core is agnostic, more than one arrangement of domains over it is possible, and this document names the three arrangements and the single axis that distinguishes them. It defines the types; where the untyped crossing that places them on one axis is specified is the composition spec, and which type this submission is, and why, is argued in the judges document. This document is neutral among the three.

The axis is the direction of authority over the schema. At one end, authority is distributed to the domains; at the other, it is held by a single schema; and the untyped type is the coordinate that runs between them.

## Domain-specific kernel

Content over the core for one subject: its own schema, its own floors, standing alone. A physics kernel, a nutrition kernel. This is the base unit, and it is a source of content rather than a composition of them.

## Bottom-up meta kernel

An arrangement assembled from pre-existing independent domain kernels that keep their own schemas, joined at the untyped type, with a composite selecting which domain kernels to include. Authority lives in the domain kernels; the meta kernel is a chooser and a join point; and a shared schema, to whatever extent one exists, is an emergent residue of use, as retail forks of repeated crossings accumulate into wholesale ones. This is federation. There can be more than one domain kernel for the same subject, built by different communities under different commitments, and the composite chooses among rivals, which is competition at the domain layer.

## Top-down meta kernel

An arrangement that starts from one schema designed to span the domains and instantiates the domains as regions within it. Authority lives in the single schema; the domains are carved from a whole rather than federated from parts; and composition is native, because everything was one type system from the start and no crossing is needed internally. The claim a top-down meta kernel makes is bounded and checkable: not that one schema can type all subjects, which is imperial and unfalsifiable, but that one designed schema spans these particular domains, which is provable by exhibiting it.

## The untyped type as the axis

The three are points on one axis rather than three separate boxes, and the untyped type is what makes the axis continuous. A top-down meta kernel is the point where the domains are regions of one schema, so every internal crossing is an identity, the degenerate case of a crossing in which nothing is lost because the two floors coincide. A bottom-up meta kernel is the point where the domains are sovereign kernels and every crossing is a lossy untyped bridge. Each converges toward the other as schemas merge or relax: a bottom-up federation whose retail forks accumulate into a fully shared schema becomes top-down, and a top-down schema relaxed into independent schemas joined at the untyped type becomes bottom-up. The composition spec defines how the crossing works; this is where the three types sit on it.

## The trade-off, not a hierarchy

Neither end is superior, and the axis is a choice of regime rather than a ranking. Top-down buys native composition and coherence at the cost of one schema everyone must accept, which suits a single author or a team building a wide but unified analysis, where one schema is affordable and the coherence is worth it. Bottom-up buys federation and incremental adoption at the cost of lossy crossings and weaker transferred standing, which suits a diverse community that will never agree on one schema, where the ability to compose without agreeing is worth the thinner standing that crosses. The right arrangement is the one whose costs a given situation can bear, and a submission that builds one type is building the right thing for its situation while the other type remains the right thing for a different one.
