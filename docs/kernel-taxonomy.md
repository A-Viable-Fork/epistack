# The Kernel Taxonomy

## The type, before the arrangements

The contribution is a type of epistemic infrastructure, a knowledge kernel, and the arrangements below are settings of it, so the type is named first. A knowledge kernel is three things and two markets over them. It is a sub-module that declares grounding, where types fix floors and one rule holds every claim, that a claim is as grounded as its weakest necessary support, recomputable by any party trusting no one. It is a grounding linter that checks those declared grades on every change, so the gate admits a claim only when the grade it declares is covered by the grade it earns, and the check suite is that linter run in CI. And it is shared subtrees others opt into, which open two independent markets over the one fixed core: a market of kernels, where a participant chooses which grounding to stand under and can fork off any of them at the shared untyped type, and a market of peripheries, where a participant chooses which interface to read and write through, any surface over any kernel because both speak only the read-write contract.

The two markets are independent, and that independence is the trust-view cut made into choices a participant makes: which grounding you trust is one choice, how you want to read and write it is another, and neither is bound to the other. A surface that talks only through the contract does not care which kernel answers, so surfaces and kernels mix freely. Selection and fork are the optionality, nothing is enrolled and nothing is mandated, and a market is what the optionality yields when many parties exercise it.

Both markets are built at minimum scale and unpopulated at large scale, and the honest line holds on each. The fixed core is built: the grounding rule, the gate, the linter as the check suite. On the trust market, the fork is a built exit right, the untyped type and the content-addressed re-point in the tree, and many rival kernels federating is adoption. On the interface market, the contract is live and makes surface and kernel independent today, the shell is one surface over the trust and the register view is a second, and many third-party surfaces is adoption. So the type exposes both markets and lowers the barrier to entry; it does not claim either market is crowded.

## The arrangements, as settings of the trust market

Because the core is agnostic, more than one arrangement of domains over it is possible, and the arrangements are settings on the trust market's axis, the direction of authority over the schema. This document names the three arrangements and that axis. It defines the types; where the untyped crossing that places them on one axis is specified is the composition spec, and which arrangement this submission builds, and why, is argued in the judges document. This document is neutral among the three.

The axis is the direction of authority over the schema. At one end, authority is distributed to the domains; at the other, it is held by a single schema; and the untyped type is the coordinate that runs between them. The interface market is orthogonal to this axis and holds identically at every point on it, because a federated kernel and a single-schema one expose the same read-write contract to the same surfaces.

## Domain-specific kernel

Content over the core for one subject: its own schema, its own floors, standing alone. A physics kernel, a nutrition kernel. This is the base unit, and it is a source of content rather than a composition of them.

## Bottom-up meta kernel

An arrangement assembled from pre-existing independent domain kernels that keep their own schemas, joined at the untyped type, with a composite selecting which domain kernels to include. Authority lives in the domain kernels; the meta kernel is a chooser and a join point; and a shared schema, to whatever extent one exists, is an emergent residue of use, as retail forks of repeated crossings accumulate into wholesale ones. This is federation. There can be more than one domain kernel for the same subject, built by different communities under different commitments, and the composite chooses among rivals, which is competition at the domain layer.

## Top-down meta kernel

An arrangement that starts from one schema designed to span the domains and instantiates the domains as regions within it. Authority lives in the single schema; the domains are carved from a whole rather than federated from parts; and composition is native, because everything was one type system from the start and no crossing is needed internally. The claim a top-down meta kernel makes is bounded and checkable: not that one schema can type all subjects, which is imperial and unfalsifiable, but that one designed schema spans these particular domains, which is provable by exhibiting it.

## The untyped type as the axis

The three are points on one axis rather than three separate boxes, and the untyped type is what makes the axis continuous. A top-down meta kernel is the point where the domains are regions of one schema, so every internal crossing is an identity, the degenerate case of a crossing in which nothing is lost because the two floors coincide. A bottom-up meta kernel is the point where the domains are standalone kernels and every crossing is a lossy untyped bridge. Each converges toward the other as schemas merge or relax: a bottom-up federation whose retail forks accumulate into a fully shared schema becomes top-down, and a top-down schema relaxed into independent schemas joined at the untyped type becomes bottom-up. The composition spec defines how the crossing works; this is where the three types sit on it.

## The trade-off, not a hierarchy

Neither end is superior, and the axis is a choice of regime rather than a ranking. Top-down buys native composition and coherence at the cost of one schema everyone must accept, which suits a single author or a team building a wide but unified analysis, where one schema is affordable and the coherence is worth it, and which is why it is the arrangement this submission builds: a competition asking for one checkable analysis and a sole author with no independent authorities to federate jointly select the top-down setting. Bottom-up buys federation and incremental adoption at the cost of lossy crossings and weaker transferred standing, which suits a diverse community that will never agree on one schema, where the ability to compose without agreeing is worth the thinner standing that crosses, and a different entrant, many labs federating knowledge bases they already keep, should choose bottom-up from the identical knowledge kernel type. The right arrangement is the one whose costs a given situation can bear, and a submission that builds one type is building the right thing for its situation while the other type remains the right thing for a different one.
