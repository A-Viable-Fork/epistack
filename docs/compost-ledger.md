# The Compost Ledger

This is the record of what the finished submission survived: the framings, mechanisms, and self-gradings we took seriously while building EpiStack and then killed for a characterized reason. It is an exhibit, not a narrative. A reader can audit the kills here rather than take the polished structure on trust, which is the same discipline the kernel applies to claims, applied to the process that produced it. Every entry names the approach at its strongest, the specific thing that killed it, and what would bring it back. The entries are ordered by how much the kill shaped the final design. It applies to the reader the same subtraction the kernel applies to a claim: do not take the result on trust, audit the kills.

The first fifteen entries are the build's early compost. The last seven are from the final design and assembly stretch.

---

**1. The contribution is the tool.**
The submission first centered on the artifacts, the interfaces and the worked cases, on the reasonable premise that a tooling competition wants a working tool, so the strongest thing to show is the best epistemic tool you can build. An LLM-as-judge audit demoted this: the artifacts read as smaller than the ambition, because the tool's value was illegible without the structure underneath it, and a thick periphery would be claiming to be a product when the actual contribution is the protocol. The contribution relocated to the kernel, and the periphery became minimal-by-design, an existence proof that the tool is cheap once the structure is checkable.
Reactivation: none, structurally. The checkable core is the contribution; a polished periphery is a demonstration surface, not the thing.

**2. The submission's genre is a critique with counterexamples.**
The competition explicitly lists critique-with-counterexamples as a valid submission shape, and the submission genuinely does critique synthesis, so filing it under that named category looked both honest and safe. It died on two counts: that category is a stepping-stone the competition says is unlikely to win the largest prizes, and filing there misstates a thesis that is constructive rather than negative. The genre became the protocol, with the critique of synthesis kept as the opening movement rather than the whole.
Reactivation: none. The critique still opens the argument; only its use as the submission's genre died.

**3. Let the judge infer the protocol from the cases.**
Presenting three dense cases and trusting the judge to reconstruct the protocol underneath is a defensible economy, since the cases do demonstrate the engine. The audit showed judges do not make that inference on their own, so the burden had to move off the reader. The fix was the arc: build the general structure first, the synthesis critique, the floor, the kernel, the taxonomy, and show the artifact as an instance of it, doing the inference for the judge rather than hoping they perform it.
Reactivation: none. The arc carries the inference.

**4. Uplift is a catalog of interfaces plus a list of capabilities.**
The first epistemic-uplift document argued two things side by side: the lenses the structure unlocks, and the capabilities the kernel supplies. This was true and it covered the ground, so it was a reasonable shape. It was demoted for arguing two things weakly instead of one thing hard: the real uplift is the composition of the two, and specifically the market a checkable substrate enables, many views over shared trust. The document was rebuilt around the equation, trust composed with view, with the lenses and capabilities as the two halves of one claim.
Reactivation: none. The composition equation is the thesis, and the list form buried it.

**5. One schema for everything.**
The submission implied it might be the universal schema, and since it genuinely spans three domains that share nothing in their subject matter, the generality claim was tempting and looked impressive. It died because a schema for all subjects is imperial and unfalsifiable, and a careful judge distrusts exactly that. The claim scoped down to a top-down meta kernel spanning these three domains, which is checkable by exhibiting it, and the untyped type opened the bottom-up alternative for the case the scoped claim does not cover.
Reactivation: none for the universal claim. The scoped, exhibitable claim is the honest survivor.

**6. A single root of truth.**
When we built the documentation dependency chain, we named the judges document as its root, the canonical source everything derives from, which is the natural shape for a dependency graph. It was backwards. The judges document is a compilation that points at where truth lives, so it is the furthest-derived leaf, not the root. Truth relocated to a set of sources, one per independent commitment, identified by a fork test, with the judges document as the leaf that compiles them.
Reactivation: none, structurally. The architecture is a set of independent commitments, so a single root was a category error.

**7. Fallible AI in the periphery.**
The boundary was first drawn as trusted core versus fallible AI, which reads cleanly and matches the intuition that AI is the risky part. It implied the architecture treats humans and machines differently by design, which it does not: the core checks a claim's structure, not the claimant's nature. The framing became producer-agnosticism, checkable-core versus fallible-producer where a producer is anything that emits claims, and any differential treatment of agents became a free parameter a community configures rather than an architectural given.
Reactivation: none. Nothing about a crossing depends on whether a producer is human or a model; agent policy lives in the free tier of the register.

**8. Every atlas entry needs two clones.**
We stated a single admission rule for the atlas, that an entry earns its place only with at least two clones diverging at a real coordinate, which was correct for the first entry we wrote, a family discriminator whose whole content is the divergence. Applied to the whole atlas it was a category error, because a grounding transformation like a measurement terminating at its floor is complete with one instance, since its content is the termination, not a contrast. The atlas split into two registers: a transformation basis admitted solo on type-correctness, and discriminators admitted in pairs on a departure.
Reactivation: none. The two-clone rule is correct, but only for discriminators.

**9. Claim the uplift of the best other submissions.**
The market argument suggests that if the architecture enables a market of tools, it should be credited with the uplift of the best tools built over it, which follows if you are genuinely an enabler. Stated as a bid it was dangerous and partly circular: it reads as grabbing competitors' merit by architectural fiat, and it overclaims uplift from peripheries that do not yet exist. It was replaced by a critique offered as a finding, that the benchmark measures one axis of uplift and omits the one the submission contributes on, with the enabled-not-guaranteed bound holding so the claim is to enablement, not to a realized market.
Reactivation: none. The finding framing is honest and the inheritance framing overclaims; only the enablement is demonstrable.

**10. Multiple proofs are convergence, like multiple measurements.**
Developing the knowledge-production axis, we first treated many proofs of one theorem as the composition that subtracts the knower, by direct analogy to many measurements converging. The analogy was wrong on a fine point: a proof is checkable, so the knower is subtracted by verification of a single proof plus its review, not by redundancy across many. The definition split into two warrants, verification where a claim is checkable, with the proof as the pure case, and convergence where it is not, with the measurement as the case, which also closed the skeptic's reading that the composition is only error-averaging.
Reactivation: none. Verification and convergence are two distinct warrants, and the many-proofs framing conflated them.

**11. "Domain kernel" is a misnomer.**
Early in resolving the kernel confusion, we ruled that "kernel" should name only the domain-agnostic core, so "domain kernel" was wrong and should be "domain store." The taxonomy then rehabilitated the term: a domain-specific kernel is a legitimate type. So the planned vocabulary sweep changed from replacement to disambiguation, the core is the kernel, this submission's domains are regions of its top-down meta kernel, and "domain kernel" is reserved for the sovereign units of a bottom-up federation.
Reactivation: none. The disambiguation stands; the blanket-misnomer ruling was too strong.

**12. Automatic schema translation across the external contract.**
For foreign peripheries writing to the kernel, automatic translation of another tool's schema into ours would make interoperation seamless, which is what a frictionless contract seems to want. It was refused permanently, not deferred: automatic translation reintroduces exactly the trusted authority the kernel exists to remove, since something would have to be trusted to translate. Foreign structured output still crosses through the gate, and foreign prose crosses only as an owned cast, one signed judgment per claim.
Reactivation: none for automatic translation. It is structurally rejected; the owned cast is the only honest crossing.

**13. The choline fork as an atlas pattern.**
The choline good-versus-TMAO split, one nutrient routed to opposite effects by a subsystem, is a real and clean structural shape, and it was a genuine candidate for an atlas discrimination pattern. It was held out because it had only one clone, and admitting it would have been the exact forcing the atlas discipline forbids. It stays a node in the eggs case rather than a pattern.
Reactivation: a second case instantiating the one-input-two-subsystems-opposite-valence shape. Then it earns a discriminator entry.

**14. Restructure into sovereign domain kernels as future work.**
The truer and more general architecture, many independent domain kernels federating into a bottom-up meta kernel, was first deferred, on the reasoning that building it before the deadline would trade a working top-down artifact for a half-migrated one that demonstrates less. It was subsequently built: the bottom-up meta kernel runs the four cases as standalone members joined at the untyped type, each owning its schema, with independence proven adversarially. This entry is compost only as a deferral that later became built work, not a killed approach.
Reactivation: not applicable. It was built.

**15. Transcribe the three debate videos.**
The COVID case's deepest source is fifteen hours of recorded debate, and extracting it raw would be the most complete possible ingestion. We deferred it because the two expert judges watched all fifteen hours and wrote decisions that distill exactly the load-bearing arguments and their weightings, so the written decisions plus the writeup recover most of the depth from text that already exists, at a fraction of the cost.
Reactivation: if the text pass shows the videos carry structure the judges did not distill, transcription earns its cost. Until then it is deferred behind the cheaper source.

**16. The demand-side validation toll.**
In the coordination layer, validation was first designed as a demand-side toll: you pay validation work in order to get your own claim promoted through the queue. It died on the free-rider problem, because it lets contributors who do not want promotion flood low-quality claims for free and taxes only the conscientious. It became a supply-side toll: writing any claim requires validation work, so the supply of validation scales with the supply of claims automatically.
Reactivation: none. Supply-side tolling is strictly better against the flood.

**17. Standing as a currency.**
Standing was first considered as something a participant spends, stakes, and accumulates. It died because a currency creates timing games, hoarding and dumping standing at strategic moments, invites a tradable reputation market, and makes staking dangerous. Standing became a weighting rather than a currency, a computed reading of a record that is never spent, transferred, or held.
Reactivation: none. Weighting-not-currency removes the timing games by construction and keeps the commons from becoming a market.

**18. The crossing-behavior field.**
Custom types were first required to declare what a claim of that type lands as when it crosses the untyped boundary, a per-type authoring burden added to every custom kind and floor. It dissolved under one law: a type is untyped abroad unless it is shared, so there is no crossing rule to author, only the private, published, and adopted states, and a claim of an unadopted type simply arrives untyped and grounds nothing.
Reactivation: none. The untyped type already defines crossing behavior; the field was reinventing it.

**19. The three-level function, form, contract split.**
Coordination mechanisms were first described at three levels: the function, the form, and the contract as distinct layers. The middle level did no work, because form and contract behaved identically under composition and translation. It collapsed to two levels once the form was seen as the type of the contract: a function slot, and a typed contract that fills it.
Reactivation: none. Form-is-the-type-of-contract is the clean two-level structure.

**20. The log multiplier as the standing mechanism.**
The log-of-tenure multiplier was found for standing, liked, and briefly treated as the standing mechanism itself. It was an over-commitment: the log is one form filling the tenure-weighting function, and asserting that the log is the best such function is an empirical claim neither defensible here nor necessary. It survives as an illustrative form; only its promotion to the mechanism died, and the spec now fixes the concave threshold-free properties and leaves the form free.
Reactivation: none for the promotion. Which form is best is a question left to the market of forms.

**21. A single, universal parameter tier.**
The parameters register first drew one line, free versus required. The coordination layer showed the free tier itself has structure: a free parameter is static or floating, and a floating parameter must float on the community's own local state, never on federation-global state. The two-tier line refined into a three-way taxonomy, fixed, free-static, and free-floating.
Reactivation: none. The free tier is genuinely structured, and collapsing it back hides the floating-parameter distinction.

**22. "Keeps the center out" as the framing.**
The anti-capture invariants were first framed as keeping the center out, which is negation-first and adversarial, defined by the enemy it prevents. It was replaced by an affirmative statement of the same property, keeping authority distributed by keeping standing computed rather than granted.
Reactivation: none. Affirmative framing is the standard the submission holds throughout.
