# The Asymmetric Weapon

## The concept, cited

In "Guided By The Beauty Of Our Weapons" (Slate Star Codex, March 2017), Scott Alexander draws a line between symmetric and asymmetric weapons. A symmetric weapon works equally well for whoever picks it up: rhetoric persuades for the true cause and the false one alike, and violence wins for the just and the unjust alike, so neither favors the side that is right. An asymmetric weapon is structurally stronger in the hands of the side that is right. His example is logical debate, because logic done correctly can only prove things that are true, so a person arguing for a false conclusion is fighting the weapon itself.

This protocol is built to be an asymmetric weapon. This document states exactly what the asymmetry is, where in the mechanism it lives, and what it does not claim. Every asymmetry here is built and checkable, and the one place the mechanism stops short is stated first, as the foundation the rest rests on rather than as a concession pulled out under pressure.

## The seam, stated first: structural and semantic attenuation

The gate does not detect truth. It verifies grounding: that a claim follows from its cited support to the grade it declares, and no higher. State the boundary in two terms. The kernel performs **structural attenuation**: it drives trust in the producer to zero with respect to structure, so a claim's grade follows from its cited support by arithmetic anyone can recompute, and who produced it changes nothing. What it does not perform is **semantic attenuation**: whether a leaf measurement actually happened, whether a named checker exists, whether claimed independence is real. That is judgment about the world, and it is the community's function. The kernel's half is complete and mechanical; the community's half stays the community's, and the kernel makes it checkable rather than performs it.

The right analogy for the structural half is a proof assistant. A Lean proof does not guarantee its theorem is true in the world; it guarantees the conclusion follows from the declared axioms. Its value is not omniscience, it is localization: the axioms are named and inspectable, so the trust a reader must extend is concentrated in a small, visible place instead of diffused through an author's authority. Fudge an axiom and the proof still compiles, but the fudge now sits in a named node that anyone can open and inspect. That localization is exactly the structural attenuation the gate performs, and every asymmetry below is an instance of it: the mechanism makes each claim expose the exact place its trust lives, so a reader never has to trust the whole of a producer, only to look at one named node and decide about that.

The boundary is forced, not chosen. Any mechanism that tried to perform semantic attenuation at the claim level would have to be a semantic judge inside the kernel, and that judge is three fatal things at once. It is a reintroduced trusted producer: whatever evaluates meaning is an oracle whose judgment must be trusted, inside the machine whose whole purpose is eliminating exactly that, and its verdicts are not recomputable the way meet and join are. It is a fixed Goodhart target: the filter's variety is fixed and the adversary's is unbounded, so the filter becomes the thing optimized against, honest producers eating its false positives while adversaries learn its blind spots. And it is a centralization of the fraud surface: today a cartel forges leaf by leaf, in named falsifiable attestations, at maximum exposure per claim, whereas a claim-level semantic validator inverts that, since gaming the validator once stamps every laundered claim that passes it, compressing a distributed, contestable, forkable adversarial surface into one high-leverage target whose capture is silent. The adversary does not disappear under a semantic mechanism; it relocates to whatever judges semantics, and a fixed mechanism is a better target than a community, which has requisite variety, adapts, contests, and forks.

One precision, so the boundary is not misread as semantics-free. The kernel carries semantic commitments: the kind ceilings, the source classes with their footings, the disjoint-footprint corroboration rule. But those are the community's semantic judgments, made at parameter-setting time, which the kernel structuralizes, making them durable, explicit, and mechanically enforceable. The community sets semantic policy and performs semantic attenuation; the kernel enforces the policy as structure and performs structural attenuation. Typing is where community semantics become kernel structure.

## The representation the weapons rest on

The asymmetries are not policy layered on top of the data; they fall out of how the data is shaped. The kernel is a content-addressed claim graph. A claim's identity is the hash of its own content (`computeClaimIdentity`, RFC `docs/protocol-spec.md` Section 2.4), links reference claims by those hashes, and the store is a hash chain of states, so a past state cannot be rewritten without breaking every later link. A grounded claim's whole certification, its grades, its bindings, its checking records, the state it was verified against, and the ruleset it was decided under, is sealed into one certificate hash (RFC Sections 2.4 and 5, checked by `build/check-certificate.mjs`). Standing is therefore never a property of a claim in the abstract. It is standing at a hash: certified by a named community's ruleset against a specific state, and expiring the moment what it sealed changes.

![The EpiStack protocol stack: a claim descends from any producer through typing, gating, and the store into a community, gaining a checkable wrapper at each layer while trust in the producer attenuates from high to none, and the same wrappers are unwrapped and recomputed on the right without the producer present.](assets/protocol-stack.png)

The stack reads as encapsulation. A claim from any producer descends through the layers, typing, the gate, the store, the community, and at each layer it gains a wrapper that anyone can check: the content hash, the gate receipt, the state chain, the certificate. As it descends, the trust a reader must place in the producer attenuates from high to none, because each wrapper replaces a thing you would otherwise have to take on the producer's word with a thing you can recompute yourself. On the right of the figure the wrappers are unwrapped and recomputed with the producer absent, and every layer's check either holds or announces exactly where it fails. The producer is load-bearing at the top and irrelevant at the bottom, and nothing in between asks you to trust them.

## The weapons, each tied to its mechanism

Each asymmetry below is structural, not a rule that a maintainer could relax, because it is a consequence of the representation. Each is marked with the mechanism and the check that makes it true, and each is built.

- **The gate sees roots, not crowns (producer-agnosticism).** Admission is a deterministic function of the claim's content and the store state. Authority, resources, and reputation buy no grade, because the grade is recomputable by anyone from public structure, and a recomputation reads the claim's roots (its support) and never its crown (its author). This is asymmetric against exactly the adversary trust systems cannot handle: the resourced-but-ungrounded party, whose advantage is precisely the reputation the gate refuses to read. Built: the gate (`kernel/gate/gate.mjs`), checked by `build/check-gate.mjs`.

- **Strict satisfies loose (the passport).** A claim graded under a strict apparatus carries its grade into any kernel that pins the same type-bundle hash, and it satisfies every looser floor there, because a grade earned against more demanding rules clears a less demanding bar by construction. A claim from a looser apparatus arrives untyped at a strict border and grounds nothing until a local fork re-earns it under the strict rules. Strictness is globally exportable; looseness is stranded at every stricter frontier. Rigor gains a network advantage by structure, not by marketing. Built: the crossing and the type-bundle hash (`kernel/composition/transfer.mjs`, `kernel/schema/type-hash.mjs`), checked by `build/check-crossing.mjs` and `build/check-type-hash.mjs`.

- **Verification expires (the certificate).** A certificate is valid against exactly the state and ruleset its bundle names. When either moves, the certificate no longer reproduces, and it is detected stale by comparison, then re-earned on next access. Grounded claims survive rehash-and-recheck and keep traveling; drifted ones are flagged rather than riding on credentials that no longer hold. Trust in a person does not expire when the facts underneath change, and that is the failure mode of reputation; a hash does expire, automatically, the instant its sealed content changes. Built: the certificate hash (`certificateSeal` in `kernel/gate/gate.mjs`) and staleness by comparison (`isStale` in `kernel/composition/transfer.mjs`), checked by `build/check-certificate.mjs`.

- **Gaps must be named (forced transparency).** Incompleteness is a first-class checked object here: the sorry ledger enumerates every deferred-verification marker and the characterized-gaps machinery types the structural holes, both mechanically reproduced from the graph. Naming a gap costs the honest party nothing, because they were going to name it anyway, and it costs the overclaimer the exact thing they were trying to hide. The asymmetry is that the honest and the overclaiming party pay opposite prices for the same required disclosure. Built: the ledgers and their checks (`docs/sorry-ledger.md`, `build/check-gaps.mjs`, `build/check-characterized-gaps.mjs`).

## The adversarial test: the cartel

The strongest objection deserves its strongest form. Suppose a community pins the genuinely strict apparatus, so its type-bundle hash is satisfied and its passport travels, and then publishes formally compliant structures whose verification is fake: checking records for checks that were never run, sock-puppet checkers each claiming distinct-party independence, fabricated leaf measurements at the bottom of otherwise coherent support trees. The grades recompute cleanly, because the structure is internally consistent; the lie is in the attestations, not the arithmetic. The cartel attacks semantic attenuation, not structural, which is why the defenses below are the community's. What the mechanism does and does not do against this attack is set out below.

- **What recomputation catches, and what it cannot.** Recomputation catches arithmetic fraud: a declared grade above what the published structure actually delivers is caught as `GM-ABOVE`, deterministically, by anyone. It cannot catch attestation fraud, because the gate checks structure, not correspondence to the world. A checking record is an assertion that a check occurred, and nothing mechanical proves the checker exists or performed anything. A perfect forgery that no one ever re-checks stands. This is the seam of the whole system, the same seam named at the start: the gate verifies grounding, not truth, and a fabricated ground is still a ground until someone inspects the named node it sits in. The argument's credibility depends on conceding this before any judge has to find it.

- **What the mechanism forces instead: fraud in writing, at named addresses.** In a producer-trust system the same fraud lives in unfalsifiable reputation and stays deniable: no specific artifact is ever committed, so nothing can later be shown false. Here the cartel must publish specific falsifiable forgeries: this `checker_id`, this method, this outcome, this claimed independence, each one a permanent attributable artifact sealed into certificates and written into the immutable state chain. The forgeries cannot be quietly retracted when scrutiny arrives, because the history shows exactly what was certified and when. The cartel is forced to build and sign its own evidence locker.

- **The exposure asymmetry.** One re-performed check that fails exposes a forged record, and the consequence is total and retroactive. Contamination collapses everything resting on the exposed leaf by the weakest-link rule; every certificate that sealed the leaf is identifiable in the chain; and communities drop the pinned hash, which kills the passport ecosystem-wide, not just locally. So the cartel needs every one of its forgeries to survive every possible spot-check forever, while the honest community needs nothing to survive, because it has nothing to hide. Fraud requires perfection under permanent audit exposure; honesty requires no maintenance at all.

- **The passport herds fraud into its most catchable form.** The cartel forges at the strict tier, because only the strict passport travels, but the strict apparatus demands more attestations per claim, deeper support, and demonstrated disjoint footprints. So strictness maximizes the forgery surface: more named, falsifiable artifacts and more tripwires per laundered claim. The system does not prevent the cartel from forging; it herds the cartel into committing maximum-exposure fraud in order to gain any reach at all.

- **The frontier.** Who re-performs checks, what a challenge costs, what standing consequences fall on an exposed forger, and whether checker identity gains cryptographic teeth so a sock puppet cannot claim to be a real checker, these are the coordination layer (`docs/coordination-layer-spec.md`), named as the specified frontier, not asserted as built. The built mechanism guarantees that the forgeries are localized, falsifiable, immutable, and terminally consequential if audited. The auditing economics, who audits and why it pays, are the frontier.

- **The specified answer to the auditing economics: the shared compost pile.** The coordination layer's constructive extension here is a federated record of what died: killed approaches, failed groundings, retracted claims, and exposed forgeries as one entry type among them. Each entry is hash-pinned, grounded in its evidence, and carries its reactivation condition, for a dead approach the result that would revive it, for an exposed community the fork-and-re-grounding that would re-earn a pinnable hash. This amortizes detection: one community's audit protects every community forever, because the exposed hash is pinned to the incident and any kernel can check an incoming passport against the record. And it inverts the participation economics. Nobody maintains a blacklist out of duty, but everyone wants shared negative knowledge, because a dead end recorded once saves every community from re-dying the same death, so the fraud records ride on infrastructure the federation feeds for positive-sum reasons. Entries are claims: they ground or they are forum-tier noise, they are contestable by undercut, and the pile is forkable like everything else, so a captured pile is abandoned rather than obeyed. The single-kernel seed is built (the store's exclusion record in `kernel/store/exclusion.js`, and this submission's own `docs/compost-ledger.md`); the federated pile is its coordination-layer extension, specified, the same way standing extends grade. The line is exact: the positive market shares what stands (certificates, passports), the negative market shares what died (the shared compost), and the asymmetric weapon needs both, so that honest work travels and so does exposure.

- **The asymmetry, restated for this attack.** The claim is not that fraud is impossible. It is that fraud must sign its name at a hash-sealed address and then survive every audit forever, in a federation where any single exposure is amortized across all communities permanently, while honesty survives every audit for free and inherits every recorded dead end as a gift.

## Close

The weapon is the certificate that expires and the fraud that must sign its name. Honest content survives rehashing and rechecking by anyone, because nothing in it depends on who produced it. Fudged content is localized by its own hash to the exact node where it breaks, and it stays there, signed and dated, waiting for one audit. That is the asymmetry, and it is structural.
