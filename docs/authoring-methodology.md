# The Authoring Methodology

## The question, and why the distinction does not hold

No human typed a line in this repository. The cases were generated by AI agents, validated against primary sources by deep research, audited by an adversarial model, and translated into the schema by a deterministic script, with a human supplying direction and judgment at the discharge points and typing none of the result. So "hand built" is not an imprecise description of the authoring. It is the inverse of what happened.

The authorship fact is not the point, though, and resting on it invites the sharper objection: if an agent wrote the cases, an agent could have written cases tuned to flatter the kernel. The real answer is one level down, and it is the submission's own thesis. The kernel checks the claim, not the agent. It is producer-agnostic by construction ([`docs/trust-and-view.md`](trust-and-view.md), and the corollary in [`docs/what-stands-without-trust.md`](what-stands-without-trust.md)), so who or what emitted a claim does not change what the claim earns, and "hand built versus agent built" is not a distinction the kernel makes. A reader reaching for "hand built" has noticed the producer-agnosticism and given it the wrong name.

## Why no producer can set the verdict

Producer-agnosticism is a slogan until three properties in the tree make the authored input structurally unable to fix the verdict. These are what a skeptic should check, because they are what convert "who wrote it does not matter" into a fact rather than a hope.

The translation is deterministic and changes no grounding verdict. `build/translate-trellis.mjs` is a pure translator from the authored trellis to the schema records, two runs byte-identical, and its stated invariant is that a node's earned grade reproduces the floor its terminal already earned. The authoring proposes a grade; it cannot set one.

The verdicts are computed, not authored. The authoring supplies claims, sources, and links. The kernel supplies the earned grades, the contamination reprice, the robustness single-points-of-failure, and the reconciliation crux. An author who wanted the LHC convergence to read robust could not make it so, because reifying the shared dependency and running the contamination rule reprices it mechanically, and the COVID crux resolves to the priors by the reconciliation computation over the graph, not by anyone placing it there. The COVID trellis records exactly this transition in its own words, that what the trellis once located by hand the engine now computes and checks.

The proposed grade is checked, not trusted. The gate checks a claim's declared grade against what it earns, and a disagreement is logged as a finding. The COVID corpus states it as its own invariant at the top of the file: the source write-up is canonical for what the claims and sides are, never for the grade they earn.

The cases were chosen to demonstrate the gate admitting well-grounded claims, so every checking record in them confirms. The gate's declining behavior, an over-declared grade held back, a superseded reference refused, an unearned crossing left untyped, is exercised separately: in the compose-gate demonstration and the gate oracle's decline cases (`build/check-gate.mjs`, `build/gate-demo.mjs`).

So the producer, human or agent, chooses what to say and the kernel decides what it is worth. That division is the point of producer-agnosticism, and it holds against the human author as much as against the agents.

## Generation separated from verification, by distinct parties

The separation of generation from verification is real here because the parties are distinct, which is the one thing a single-model pipeline cannot claim. Generation and architecture were one agent. Construction in the repository was a separate execution path. Validation against primary sources was deep research run against the named documents, the Worobey and Liu and Crits-Christoph studies for COVID, the LHC safety assessments, the nutrition literature for eggs. Adversarial audit was a further model. The human was none of these, sitting at the discharge points deciding what passed under the trellis constraints. No system generated and then graded its own output.

The trellis constraints bound the generators against tuning to flatter, and two are load-bearing here: the constructed question set is revisable and submits to its own revision, and a channel an outside reader names that the set omits is a falsification of completeness, not a nuisance. The second invites exactly the attack a cherry-picking objection would make, and treats a hit as a defect to record rather than a critique to deflect.

## What is authored, and the honest floor

Producer-agnosticism removes the producer's power over the verdict. It does not remove the producer's choice of what to author, and pretending otherwise would be the blur this submission refuses. The selection of cases, and of which claims populate them, is the workflow's, and no computation makes that selection representative. This is the honest residual, and it is bounded three ways. The selection is exposed to falsification, because an omitted channel is a defect a reader is invited to name. The three cases are deliberately unlike, a measurement floor in physics, a contested estimate in epidemiology, a declaration in nutrition, chosen to stress the schema across floor types rather than to flatter it. And the submission already concedes it spans these three, exhibited in the tree, not all subjects. The selection is authored, exposed, and bounded; the verdicts over it are computed and unriggable.

The workflow that produced the cases is also not the automated ingestion agent, and the difference is the built-versus-specified line. The fully automated ingestion agent is Stage 2 in [the status ledger](status-ledger.md), specified and not built. The human here was necessary as judgment and direction, not as typist, so what is demonstrated is a human-AI workflow hitting the typed target on three cases, and what is designed and deferred is the agent that would hit the same target without a human at each discharge point. The strong claim and its floor stand together: no human types this repository, and it does not matter who does, because the architecture checks claims and not their authors; what a human still supplies is judgment at the discharge points, and removing even that is Stage 2, specified and not built.

## The repository as its own instance

The three cases demonstrate producer-agnosticism across three domains. The repository's own authoring demonstrates it once more, a graph produced by many hands and no hands, whose verdicts none of the producers could set. That is why the "hand built" reading is not merely inaccurate but backwards: the authoring is the thesis running on itself, and the fact that a reader cannot tell from the verdicts who or what produced the claims is not a gap in the account, it is the result.
