# EpiStack and The Knowledge Game: Submission Overview

EpiStack is a minimal shared structure on which independent communities build knowledge that composes. The Knowledge Game is its first app: a feed you steer over claims you check. Both exist and run today. The fastest way to evaluate it: the checks rerun in about two minutes, every grade recomputes from the public graph, and the app installs from a URL. This sits downstream of [the minimum constitution](the-minimum-constitution.md): the accounting basis for trust was well-calibrated, its regime shifted, and rebasing follows. What follows is where that shift shows, each place named with the structure that rebases it, so the six pairs below are six readings of one regime change rather than six separate attacks.

## The critiques, each with its counterexample

**A synthesis is one viewpoint asking for trust, and it stands or falls wholesale.** Reports, reviews, and AI summaries concentrate reasoning into a document; when any part is wrong, the whole document and everyone who trusted it are wrong together. The counterexample is the typed claim graph: every claim independently typed, hashed, and graded from its own support, so failure is isolable to the claim that failed. The eggs corpus is the demonstration: nutritional epidemiology, walked claim by claim through the gate, sorts into claims that ground to their floors where the evidence supports them, contested weighings held at the forum tier, and honestly characterized gaps, each gap carrying the study that would close it, which no synthesis of that literature has ever shown you.

**Credibility has ridden on who said it, and capable generation is the regime change that retires that heuristic.** Fluent, confident text is now free to produce, so producer-based trust (the journal, the expert, the model) is a collapsing foundation. The counterexample is producer-agnosticism: the gate recomputes a claim's standing from its support structure, and who proposed it never enters the computation. Knowledge, in this protocol's working definition, is what remains when trust in the producer is turned all the way down, and a claim carried by real evidence reaches full standing even from an anonymous key.

**Feeds optimize for their owners, and placement comes to stand in for credibility.** Every major platform welds together what you see and what seems true, with the objective function hidden and not yours. The counterexample is the app: the ranking objective is a visible vector the reader sets, every card answers "why am I seeing this," turning ranking off drops to a declared deterministic order, behavioral observation is opt-in and off by default, and a mechanical check proves ranking cannot move a grade, fuzzing arbitrary objectives and asserting every grade byte-identical.

**Platforms own the trust they host, and ownership is the lever that turns.** APIs get priced into walls; communities and their histories are hostages. The counterexample is unownable structure: the graph is public and content-addressed, any client with the snapshot has every capability our own app has (a minimal independent client reproduces the full capability set in CI, reading and writing), a community is a portable kernel shared as a small card, and when a community's governance is captured, adopters fork it, provenance intact, capture visible for everyone to price.

**Institutions deciding in contested domains cannot defend their reasoning.** They commission a report and inherit its opacity. The counterexample is adoption-as-parameters: an institution adopts a community's explicit parameterization, one auditable hash, and recomputes the grades itself, accountable for a parameter choice it can name and defend rather than for conclusions it could only trust. The LHC corpus shows the shape: safety claims visibly grounded in astronomical observation rather than theoretical confidence, checkable by anyone.

**Knowledge work is funded either by nothing or by capture.** Volunteer commons burn out; token schemes let money buy credibility. The counterexample is a market layer that references grades and provably cannot move them: no protocol token exists, reading and recomputing are free by construction, operators sell verification and warranty over a commons none of them can enclose, and contributors are paid from usage auditable against reference counts anyone can recompute. The one financial constraint fixed at protocol level: penalties trigger on provable fault only, never on being wrong or unpopular.

## The protocol, in a paragraph

EpiStack is a typed claim graph with a gate. A claim enters a community's kernel only if it grounds: its type is one the community defined, its support earns its grade under a fixed recurrence, and the whole computation reruns on anyone's machine. Communities are kernels; each freezes its semantic policy (what kinds of claims exist, what evidence can earn what) into checkable structure, named by one governance-hash. Between communities, claims travel freely but standing does not: an imported claim arrives untyped at the floor and re-earns under local judgment, so shared meaning is exactly shared hash and nothing composes by accident. Everything above standing, ordering, teaching, discussion, rendering, is a competitive market of views over structure nobody owns. The repository demonstrates all of it: working corpora for the competition's three cases (black holes, eggs, pandemic origins), a mathematics kernel, a vocabulary kernel that defines the submission's own terms as graded claims, and a check suite that makes every sentence above falsifiable in minutes.

## The community layer

A community is founded by choosing parameters, not by asking permission: its types, its evidence ceilings, its admission policy, its content license, all explicit, all carried in its card, which is a small file anyone can share, mirror, or verify by hash. Communities requiring strong identity and communities welcoming anonymity compose as easily as strangers do, because identity itself is graded claims and presentation is the agent's per-act choice, from named individual down to a bare key at the floor. Discussion exists without polluting evidence: comments are a kind that carries no grade and can never be cited as support. The constitutive choices, purpose, governance, semantic judgment, validation incentives, are deliberately left to each community; the protocol standardizes only what must be shared for artifacts to compose.

## The first app: The Knowledge Game

A mobile feed reader and community founder, live as an installable web app and an Android APK, built as the protocol's first third-party client rather than a privileged one. The reader's controls are the point: a visible objective vector (learn efficiently, strongest opposition, unresolved questions, recent changes, or any weighting), an epistemic-cost report that recomputes your feed under another community's parameters ("8 of these 12 are contested elsewhere"), a type filter that always states what it is hiding, offline reading with contributions drafted and gate-checked on the device, and pending contributions rendered as visibly virtual, ghosted potentials, until a community actually admits them. Contributions travel as content-addressed bundles through an admission path that judges bundles, never the client that produced them.

The repository practicing what it preaches is the deeper demonstration: the app carries its own governance kernel, twenty claims about its own behavior (ranking cannot modify standing, profile data cannot enter a public patch, no undeclared network egress, this client holds no special capability), each entered bare at the floor and graded upward only when a named mechanical check landed, every standing computed by the gate, none asserted by hand. The commit history walks the protocol's six-stage workflow with a machine-checked stage manifest, and the first community it founded, through its own founding flow, is this competition itself: mechanical claims about the protocol grounded by the repository's own checks, and the evaluative claims, the ones this overview has been making, sitting at their honest floor, awaiting the only thing that can lift them, which is people deciding they hold. That is the invitation, and you are the community it was founded for.

## Two readers, two paths

Whether the shared structure named above is the right minimum, enough for independent work to compound and not so much that pluralism collapses into one privileged synthesis, is [the minimum constitution](the-minimum-constitution.md)'s central question, and these two paths are how to test the answer for yourself.

**Reading as a human:** start with the argument, `docs/the-climb-of-synthesis.md`, two pages, then walk one corpus room (eggs is the most visceral), then install the app and hold the feed. Reasoning: the mechanism only matters if the problem is felt first, and a reader without the argument sees a graph database where there is actually a redefinition of what it means to know something; the room makes it concrete, and the app makes it yours.

**Reading as (or with) an LLM:** point it at `llms.txt`, which orders the repository specifically to prevent flattening, then let it read and synthesize, then rerun the checks yourself. Reasoning: a synthesizer without the types collapses exactly the distinctions this system exists to draw, so the reading order defends against that; and more deeply, feeding this submission to a model is safe by design, because you never have to trust the synthesis. Anything an LLM asserts about this repository is recomputable against the public graph in minutes, which means your trust in the synthesizer can sit at the floor and the evaluation still works. That property, evaluation that survives an untrusted middleman, is the submission demonstrating itself on you, and it is the same property every institution in the fifth critique needs.
