> **Intent.** The LHC black-hole question is a closed case that rests on a large body of interacting knowledge. This trellis reconstructs that argument as a dependency graph rather than restating its conclusion. The work is to show *why* the case is settled: which arguments are theory-dependent, which form the empirical anchor that survives removing the new physics, where the naive anchor had a gap and how it was patched, and which arguments share assumptions and so must not be counted as independent confirmations. The deliverable is an accessible inference structure a non-physicist can navigate and interrogate. It discharges S-BLACKHOLES-PARTS in the hub and feeds S-SCROLLER, S-CORRELATION coverage, and S-LEGIBILITY. The validator is faithfulness to the documented record, not the author's physics intuition.

## JURISDICTIONAL BOUNDARY

This trellis reconstructs and assesses an existing argument. It does not:
- Re-derive the underlying physics or adjudicate beyond-standard-model gravity. It maps what the safety case actually argued.
- Reopen the question. The case is treated as settled; the object is to exhibit the structure of that settledness, including its weak points.
- Cover the strangelet, magnetic-monopole, or vacuum-decay scenarios except as they bear on the black-hole argument's structure. Each is a separate argument with its own dependencies.

### Child Tier 0: Non-negotiable

`[Empirical / Methodological]` Every load-bearing claim about the safety case is grounded in a primary source (LSAG report, Giddings-Mangano, CERN), not in physics intuition. The reconstruction distinguishes theory-dependent arguments from the empirical anchor as different kinds of evidence. *Falsification: a load-bearing claim with no source, or a reconstruction that treats Hawking evaporation and the cosmic-ray argument as the same kind of evidence.*

### Child Tier 1: The reconstruction principle

`[Methodological]` The safety conclusion is represented as a dependency graph. Nodes are claims; edges are "depends on assumption X." The empirical anchor is defined as the subgraph that still supports the conclusion after every beyond-standard-model assumption is removed. *Falsification: a flat list of arguments with no dependency edges, or a claimed anchor that does not actually survive removal of the new-physics assumptions.*

### Child Tier 2: Methodology

`[Methodological]` Reconstruct why, not that. Flag arguments that share an assumption and must not be counted as independent. Note the weakest and most speculative points honestly. Render the structure accessible to a non-physicist. *Falsification: "experts agreed" or "CERN says so" as a terminal justification; correlated arguments presented as independent confirmations; weak points omitted.*

## RECONSTRUCTED STRUCTURE (VERIFIED AGAINST SOURCES, 2026-06-24)

Checked against BH-01, BH-02, BH-03. The structure the hypothesis predicted holds. Corrections and refinements are marked.

- **Top claim.** The LHC will not produce a black hole that endangers Earth.
- **Argument A, Hawking evaporation.** Any micro black hole disintegrates immediately, so it has no time to accrete (CERN FAQ states this for the black-hole case). *Depends on:* Hawking radiation being correct. *Status:* theory-dependent. The technical case deliberately does not lean on it; Giddings-Mangano assume the black hole is stable precisely to test the worst case without relying on evaporation.
- **Argument B, cosmic-ray on Earth and Sun.** Cosmic rays have struck Earth and the Sun at center-of-mass energies above the LHC for billions of years without catastrophe. *Gap (confirmed):* a cosmic-ray collision is with a stationary target, so any black hole produced is boosted and fast; it would pass through Earth or the Sun and escape rather than be captured. Earth's survival alone therefore does not bound the dangerous slow-capture case.
- **Argument C, dense-object closure (Giddings-Mangano, and in the LSAG report).** White dwarfs and neutron stars are dense enough to stop and capture even fast black holes. If stable black holes that accrete dangerously existed, cosmic rays would have seeded them in these bodies and catalyzed their decay on timescales incompatible with their observed lifetimes. So the dangerous regime, accretion faster than the Sun's natural lifetime, is ruled out. *Status:* empirical, robust to the new physics, built on first-principles well-tested laws. Closes B's gap.
- **Empirical anchor.** B repaired by C. This subgraph supports the top claim with no appeal to Hawking radiation or to the specific beyond-standard-model model. *Refinement from the LSAG report:* this same cosmic-ray anchor is shared across all four hypothetical-object scenarios (black holes, strangelets, magnetic monopoles, vacuum bubbles), not the black-hole case alone.
- **Correlation flags (two, both confirmed).** First: Argument A and any "the new physics itself implies safety" line share the assumption that our high-energy physics is right, so they are not independent confirmations; the independent line is the empirical anchor. Second, and sharper: the institutional layer is only partly independent. Mangano co-authored both the foundational accretion paper (BH-02) and the official LSAG review (BH-01), and the three physics societies endorsed that same review. The endorsements are not three fresh analyses.
- **Settled versus performed.** A reading that stops at "CERN and the LSAG concluded it is safe, and three societies agreed" performs settling, and overstates the independence of that chain. The settledness actually rests on the empirical anchor's robustness to theoretical uncertainty, specifically the dense-object closure. Exhibiting that, and being honest about the partial independence of the endorsement chain, is the demonstration.
- **Weakest points (S-BH-WEAK, advanced).** The Lorentz-invariance axiom that LSAG asserts without a number; the partial independence above; residual assumptions in C (that cosmic-ray-produced and LHC-produced black holes are relevantly similar, and that the relevant astronomical populations and lifetimes are as believed); the separate monopole, strangelet, and vacuum-decay arguments; and Eric Johnson's framing critique (BH-05) that the risk question can be insulated by how it is posed.

## DIFFERENTIAL FRAMING IS THE SOURCE'S OWN (2026-06-24)

The full LSAG text shows the "how does the LHC differ from cosmic rays, and is each difference closed" structure is the report's own spine, not a frame imposed in reconstruction. Section 1 names that cosmic-ray products have different velocities than accelerator products, so the circumstances are not directly comparable, and asks whether Earth's immunity to cosmic rays also applies to accelerator collisions. Section 4 calls the velocity difference "one significant difference between cosmic-ray collisions with a body at rest and collisions at the LHC." The dense-object argument is the closure of that named difference. This licenses rebuilding the artifact as a disanalogy tree (S-BH-TREE) on faithfulness grounds.

LSAG also names its own load-bearing axiom: reference 4 states that the cosmic-ray comparison assumes the validity of special relativity, tested for the relevant velocities, with no number attached. That is the toggle node, sitting in the source.

## VACUUM BRANCH (SECOND WORKED INSTANCE, S-BH-VACUUM)

The exclusion engine, grant a hostile premise and propagate it to an observable that is already excluded, run on the vacuum rather than black holes. Two readings, two closures, which is itself the finding that the engine generalizes.

- **Vacuum decay (the standard reading).** LSAG Section 3 closes it by the direct cosmic-ray argument with no velocity patch: if the LHC could nucleate a lower-vacuum bubble so could cosmic rays, such bubbles would have consumed the visible universe billions of years ago, and the universe still exists. No dense-object refinement is needed because a bubble expands at light speed regardless of seed velocity, so the capture gap that forced argument C for black holes does not arise. Different closure shape, and the shape difference is real: the velocity gap is black-hole-specific.
- **Vacuum as a crystal-like medium (the sharper hostile premise).** Grant that the vacuum can take sustained damage because it is always the same place. This requires a preferred location, hence a preferred frame, hence Lorentz violation. It closes twice over. Kinematically, the LHC point is not fixed in any cosmological frame; the Earth, solar system, and galaxy sweep it through space at hundreds of km/s, so "always the same place" holds only in the lab frame, which is not special. Empirically, "vacuum as a structured medium" is birefringence, which accumulates over cosmological baselines; comparative spectral polarimetry of distant sources bounds the relevant photon-sector Lorentz violation at 2x10^-32 (BH-06), and SME analyses push energy-linear variation orders of magnitude past the Planck scale (BH-07). The premise predicts a polarization rotation we would have seen and do not.
- **Terminal type.** The black-hole branch ends at neutron-star survival; the vacuum branch ends at the non-birefringence of the sky. Two branches, two unrelated bodies of data, one repeated maneuver. The generalization is shown, not asserted.
- **Residual open case (flagged, not closed).** A genuinely Lorentz-invariant cumulative effect, not tied to a preferred frame, would evade both the kinematic argument and the birefringence bound. No such mechanism has been exhibited, and "damage that accumulates at a location without a preferred frame" is close to a contradiction, but that is a conjecture, not a closure. The branch closes for the preferred-frame realization and stays open for the frame-independent one. Conditional carried as a conditional.
- **What stays out.** The author's own vacuum-stability calculation. The record closes the branch harder than a bespoke derivation would, and with citable numbers. The calculation is a separate object for a separate trellis, judged by physics standards.
