> **Intent.** The COVID-origins question is a live adversarial dispute that does not terminate on a measurement the universe has already evaluated. It terminates on irreducible priors: reference-class choices, base-rate estimates, and channel-trust judgments that the two camps already share most of the evidence over and still diverge on. This trellis reconstructs that dispute as a dependency structure and does three things the sibling black-holes leaf does not. It locates the cruxes, the specific axioms two rational agents must weight differently to land where each lands. It prices each crux, showing how much of the other side's strongest evidence each weighting has to discount to survive, because a branch on an irreducible-prior case is exposed rather than closed and honest exposure prices each side. And it refuses the verdict: which origin is true is a Tier 0 veto, not a claim this work asserts. The deliverable is the disagreement's structure with each side priced. The validator is faithfulness to the documented record, claim attribution traced to source, not the author's read of the virology. What gets killed: any node that adjudicates origins, and any graph that draws a genuine evidential imbalance as fake even-handedness. What gets logged: each crux with its terminal type, each side's discount price, and the open obligations where the pricing itself is contested.

## JURISDICTIONAL BOUNDARY

This trellis reconstructs and prices a dispute. It does not:
- Adjudicate COVID-19 origins. The verdict is not a claim this work asserts. Tier 0 veto inherited from the hub.
- Re-derive the virology, epidemiology, or genetics. It maps which claims each camp made and traces them to source, not whether the underlying science is correct.
- Resolve whether Bayesian analysis is the right tool for this problem. Several participants (Stansifer, Van Treuren, the statmodeling commentary) doubt it is. This trellis treats the analyses as objects to be compared for shared inputs, not as oracles to be averaged.
- Settle the politics. The institutional and subject-formation layer (Layer 0) is reconstructed as a generator of rational disagreement and a corruption of the shared channel, never as a claim about the existence of the fact. The virus had an origin.

These are legitimate questions. They require different infrastructure.

- **Normative floor (from the hub).** No element may carry more persuasive weight than evidential weight. No claim that the prize and the truth are one target. No verdict on a mind-independent fact grounded in usefulness rather than evidence.

### Child Tier 0: Non-negotiable

`[Value Commitment]` No node adjudicates origins. The two positions are represented without collapsing them into a consensus node. Every load-bearing attribution ("camp X asserted Y") is grounded in a primary source, not in the author's read of the science. *Falsification: a sentence that states or implies which origin is true as this work's conclusion; or a structure that resolves the dispute into one settled answer; or a load-bearing attribution with no source.*

### Child Tier 1: The crux-location principle

`[Methodological]` The dispute is represented as a dependency structure whose terminals are typed. A terminal is one of: an irreducible-by-choice prior (a reference-class or base-rate selection no fact resolves), a withheld-record terminal (a named sealed dataset would resolve it; in principle reducible, in practice gated on whether the record is unsealed), or a distributional conflict (a real conflict over which carrier the persistence floor is computed for, which survives full system understanding). **Terminal taxonomy revised 2026-06-26 (M-0.4, from the compose gate).** The original taxonomy folded sealed-resolver disputes into "irreducible-by-access channel-trust." The independent compose corrected this: a dispute with a named sealed resolver is not irreducible-in-principle, it is withheld-gated, and conflating the two understates what unsealing would settle. The flag on a withheld-record terminal means "a specific sealed door, not fog": it names the dataset whose release would collapse the disagreement, which is a different and stronger claim than "no shared evidence dissolves this." The flag on an irreducible-by-choice prior keeps the original meaning, the dispute lives here and no fact settles it. Both differ from the sibling leaf's "derivation required, go read the source model." *Falsification: a terminal drawn as a measurement when it is a prior; a withheld-record terminal drawn as irreducible when a sealed resolver is named, or as irreducible when none is; a flat list of arguments with no dependency edges; or an exclusion-tree structure forced onto a case whose terminals are not measurements.*

### Child Tier 2: Methodology (the pricing discipline)

`[Methodological]` Each crux is priced. The structure carries the asymmetry, how much each weighting has to discount to survive, not merely that a weighting exists. Generation and verification stay separated in the writeup: what is established (traced to source), what is conjectured (a structural reading), what is a leap (a pricing that is itself contested) are marked distinctly. *Falsification: a graph that hangs both positions from equally weighted axioms and implies the question is a wash; a pricing asserted without showing the discount; or a writeup that collapses established attribution, conjectured reading, and contested pricing into one confident narrative.*

## LINTER

**False Symmetry.** `[FLAG MODE, inherited, load-bearing]` *Test: does the structure hang zoonosis and lab-leak from equally weighted axioms and imply the question is a wash?* Remediation: carry the asymmetry. A branch here is exposed, not closed, and exposure prices each side. Drawing a genuine evidential imbalance as fake even-handedness is the COVID-specific form of performed settling. This fires hardest on the market-clustering crux, where the discount prices are not equal.

**Adjudication Creep.** `[KILL MODE]` *Test: does a node, a pricing, or a transition sentence state or imply which origin is true as this work's finding?* Remediation: convert to a priced crux or cut. The asymmetry lives in the discount price, never in a declared winner. Pricing how much each side discounts is permitted; converting that price into a posterior is the reader's move, not this work's.

**Motivated-Reasoning Wash.** `[KILL MODE]` *Test: does the politicization layer reduce to "both camps are politically produced, therefore it cancels"?* Remediation: this is False-Symmetry wearing Foucault, and it is also bad Foucault, whose power is asymmetric. Price the institutional positions: which trust allocation each formation makes, and what each costs. Symmetric formation is a finding to be earned per-crux, not a default.

**Metaphysical Overreach (origins).** `[KILL MODE]` *Test: does the Layer 0 or Layer 1 material slide from "the channel and the subject are politically shaped" into "there is no fact of the matter about origins"?* Remediation: keep the politics about access and subject-formation, never about the existence of the fact. ## LOCATED CRUX STRUCTURE (VERIFIED AGAINST SOURCES, 2026-06-24)

The dispute is not a flat contest of arguments. It is a small number of cruxes, each terminating on a typed irreducible prior, with the six analyses' spread propagating dominantly from two of them. Each crux below carries: the shared evidence both camps see, the two readings, the terminal type, and the discount price each side pays. Established attributions are traced to source. Conjectured readings and contested pricings are marked.

**The null both camps share.** SARS-CoV-2 had a single actual origin. Both camps work from largely the same evidence set (Stansifer notes Weissman's lab-leak analysis uses evidence substantially the same as Rootclaim's). The disagreement is therefore not mostly about which facts obtain. It is about how a few facts are weighted, and that weighting is set by priors the shared evidence does not fix.

### CV-1. The geographic prior (Wuhan and the WIV)

- **Shared evidence.** The outbreak surfaced in Wuhan. The Wuhan Institute of Virology, a major coronavirus research center, is in Wuhan. The closest known relatives (BANAL-52, RaTG13) are in Yunnan and Laos, roughly 1500 km away.
- **Zoonosis reading.** Wuhan is a large hub; the reference class of places a spillover could surface absorbs the coincidence. Weissman, arguing within the zoonosis framework, notes WIV has used Wuhan residents as negative controls for SARS-related antibodies, so Wuhan is remote from the bat reservoir. (This cuts both ways and is itself a lab-leak talking point.)
- **Lab-leak reading.** A coronavirus pandemic surfacing in the one city with a major coronavirus gain-of-function lab is a strong coincidence under zoonosis.
- **Terminal type.** Irreducible-by-choice prior (reference class). No fact resolves which reference class is correct.
- **Discount price.** The cleanest pure prior, and close to symmetric. Both sides price it by reference-class selection. Informative move: Rootclaim states that even giving Wuhan no weight, their conclusion drops only from 94% to 92% lab-leak, discounting their own strongest geographic evidence to show robustness. The price here is small and paid in the unauditable prior currency by both.

### CV-2. The market clustering (the load-bearing crux)

- **Shared evidence.** The earliest officially confirmed cases cluster on the Huanan market; the first confirmed case and roughly half of the first 40 had market connections (Peter Miller, citing the early case record). Both lineages were found at or near the market. Positive environmental samples concentrate near the southwest wildlife stalls (Worobey 2022, Pekar 2022).
- **Zoonosis reading.** The clustering is signal. Worobey and Pekar assign it a large Bayes factor; Stansifer's decision states in primary that the epidemiological proximity of the earliest indicators to a plausible animal source is the most important basis for his zoonosis verdict, with the genetic case insufficient to overcome it. His section-7.4 market-location factor, on the order of 10,000, is reported by Weissman and is the one remaining section-7 anchor not directly re-verified here.
- **Lab-leak reading.** Ascertainment bias. Deliberate screening for market connections began only on December 30 (Peter Miller concedes this date), but the market was where authorities and the SARS-1-primed scientific community looked first and hardest. Weissman 2024 published a collider-stratification model showing biased early case ascertainment fits the location data better than an all-from-market model. Remove the market Bayes factor and the zoonosis analyses flip (Weissman: every zoonosis-favoring analysis pivots on this one factor).
- **Terminal type.** Withheld-record (revised from irreducible-by-access, 2026-06-26). A named sealed resolver exists: the early case line-list (DS-EARLYCASE-DEC2019), the discriminating dataset that would settle whether the clustering is signal or ascertainment artifact. The dispute is therefore not irreducible-in-principle; it is gated on whether that record is unsealed. The compose gate caught the incumbent map typing this as irreducible-prior and corrected it: typing a sealed-resolver dispute as irreducible elides the resolver and dresses a withheld fact as a permanent standoff. In practice, while the record stays sealed, the crux still reduces to how much search distortion each camp assumes, but the flag now names the door rather than declaring fog.
- **Discount price (the sharp, partly-auditable asymmetry, where False-Symmetry fires hardest).** The two bills are not equal and not in the same currency. Zoonosis pays "trust the early case data that exists." Lab-leak pays "assume that data was distorted in a specific direction by a specific, large amount." The lab-leak bill is the heavier and the more auditable one, because the magnitude of possible ascertainment bias in the first 40 cases is constrained by the timeline: deliberate market-connection screening started December 30, after those cases. Weissman's published model is the audit attempt; the judges found it insufficient against the proximity weight. The terminal type is anchored in primary by Stansifer's own section-3 argument: a real signal and a slightly biased collection with no signal are indistinguishable from the data alone, so this crux cannot be closed by the clustering data itself, only by a prior on how much the channel was distorted. That is what makes it a withheld-record terminal rather than a measurement: the clustering data alone cannot close it, and the dataset that could is sealed. This is the crux that carries most of the spread, and pricing it as a wash would be the central False-Symmetry violation. It is not a wash: it is an exchange-rate asymmetry between a trust-the-data bill and a larger assume-the-data-was-warped bill, with the audit (the screening timeline) partly constraining the latter. Converting that asymmetry into a posterior still requires the reader to set the channel-trust prior, which this work does not do. The sealed-door framing is the sharper statement of the same asymmetry: name the resolver, price both sides against the named door, and the disagreement stops looking like irreducible fog and starts looking like a specific withheld fact with a specific cost to each side of keeping it withheld.

### CV-3. The furin cleavage site and the missing progenitor

- **Shared evidence.** COVID resembles BANAL-52 plus a 12-nucleotide furin cleavage site (FCS) absent from all ~1500 other sarbecoviruses. The FCS codes arginine as CGG-CGG (common in mammals, ~3% in this virus, rare in viruses generally). The DEFUSE proposal (2018) described inserting furin cleavage sites into such viruses. No intermediate animal host has been identified.
- **Lab-leak reading (Yuri Deigin for Rootclaim in session 2).** A clean 12-nt insertion, the mammalian CGG codons, and a grant proposing exactly this read as engineered. Absence of a found progenitor reads as suspicious.
- **Zoonosis reading (Peter Miller).** Insertions of 12 to 15 nt occur in nature (HKU1). The site is "messy" (PRRAR, not the clean RRKR a human would choose), apparently inserted via a frameshift (too clever for an engineer), and CGG appears in the virus anyway. WIV did not have BANAL-52 (discovered post-pandemic); DEFUSE was rejected and assigned the gain-of-function half to UNC. Absence reads as not-yet-found, as SARS-1's host took years.
- **Terminal type.** Interpretation prior plus absence-weighting, gated by a sealed channel. The tiebreaker (WIV's actual undisclosed virus inventory) would resolve much of it; NIH terminated WIV's subaward in 2022 for refusal to share records.
- **Discount price.** The closest-to-symmetric crux (S-CV-FCS). Each side reads the same molecular feature oppositely. Lab-leak's reading requires WIV to have held an unpublished near-perfect backbone; zoonosis's requires a low-probability natural insertion. Neither bill is auditable from outside without the sealed records. Roughly comparable price, both paid in the interpret-an-unusual-feature currency, tiebreaker sealed.

### CV-4. The base rate

- **Shared evidence.** Natural pandemics in the region occur on the order of one per 30 years (Peter Miller's count: SARS-1, plus mid-century flu pandemics). Concentrated gain-of-function research on pandemic-potential viruses is recent. A commonly cited figure is ~1/500 labs leaking a given pathogen.
- **Zoonosis reading.** The historical base rate of lab-origin pandemics is near zero; nature's is nonzero and regular.
- **Lab-leak reading.** The relevant reference class is not the historical frequency of lab pandemics but P(pandemic given this kind of research happening in this city), which the historical frequency does not capture.
- **Terminal type.** Irreducible-by-choice prior. A reference-class selection (historical frequency vs conditional-on-the-practice).
- **Discount price.** Pure prior, unauditable, paid by both in the reference-class currency. Not the locus of the spread.

### The methodology crux (where the spread's second component lives)

- **Shared object.** Rootclaim's method adds two out-of-model corrections: (b) the likelihood that a better explanation is yet to be found, and (c) the likelihood of mistakes in the estimates.
- **Lab-leak position.** These make the analysis robust.
- **Zoonosis position.** These convert epistemic uncertainty into Bayes factors. Stansifer's decision (section 3, primary) works this out: across many weak observations the log-odds undergo a random walk whose noise grows as the square root of the count, while a modest selection bias drifts linearly and overwhelms it, so a fair analysis revealing a real anomaly and a slightly biased analysis with no true signal are indistinguishable from the data. He frames the remedy as canonical evidence (consider all fingers, but only fingers) and obligate-subpart decomposition (the Drake-equation structure), which resist selection bias. The recurring critique (the "false confidence" thread, and Peter Miller's reading of Rootclaim's 10% guesstimate on the FCS insertion) is the same point: lowering data quality should not raise confidence, yet these corrections can. Weissman's analysis draws large Bayes factors from epistemic uncertainty by the same route.
- **Status.** This is an asymmetry in method, auditable in principle: does the procedure let degraded data increase confidence? Flagged, not adjudicated. It is the second component of the spread alongside CV-2, and it is shared across the lab-leak analyses (Rootclaim and Weissman), which is itself a correlated-independence finding.

### The correlated-evidence finding (the gate, S-CV-CORR)

The six analyses (zoonosis: Stansifer, Van Treuren, Filan, Alexander, Johnston, Miller; lab-leak: Rootclaim, Weissman) span roughly twenty orders of magnitude. That spread is not six independent reads. It collapses onto:
- **CV-2, the market Bayes factor.** Weissman: every zoonosis-favoring analysis pivots on the Worobey factor and would flip without it. The zoonosis analyses share this single load-bearing input.
- **The methodology crux.** The lab-leak analyses (Rootclaim, Weissman) share the out-of-model-correction structure; Stansifer notes they use substantially the same evidence.
- **A narrow source pool (requisite-variety axis).** Much of the early case data is CCP-mediated and the analyses draw on the same reporting and the same trust posture toward it.

So the apparent richness of a twenty-order-of-magnitude disagreement reduces to two or three shared inputs set differently. Both camps confirm this collapse, which is the strongest possible form of the finding: it is not asserted by this work, it is conceded by the disputants.

### Layer 0, the institutional and subject-formation layer (translated, surfaces only if the leaf weights it)

The dispute is downstream of institutions that constitute what counts as a credible source and which hypothesis counts as legitimate. The 2020-to-2021 recoding of lab-leak from conspiracy to live hypothesis, with the evidence barely moving, is the textbook instance. The formations are asymmetric, not a level marketplace: the establishment that controls the primary evidence and conducts the research has a structural interest against lab-leak (it implicates the practice and the funding); the counter-formation is constituted by distrust of that establishment, giving it an interest toward cover-up narratives. The record carries this layer directly: Weissman reports "terror among academics" on the question and researchers leaning zoonotic unable to get experts to talk; NIH terminated WIV's records subaward; the judge Stansifer credits Weissman partly via a political-coherence signal (that Weissman, unlike most lab-leak proponents, accepts climate change), and Weissman in turn reads lab-leak argument structure as resembling climate skepticism. Each camp reads the other through its formation.

The pricing discipline (Motivated-Reasoning Wash kill): this is not "both are produced, therefore symmetric." A calibration point from primary: Stansifer reports the debate itself felt like four people collaborating in good faith rather than an adversarial contest, and that Saar acted in good faith throughout. So the imbalance located here was not manufactured by partisan rancor in the room; the Layer 0 distortion bites in the broader discourse, not in this particular exchange, which is part of why the debate produced a cleaner crux structure than the public argument. The persistence-floor caps the regress for one component and not the other. Both camps share the floor abstractly (no one terminally values a worse pandemic), but they privilege different carriers, which is why even agreeing on every fact they would rank prescriptions oppositely (the shutdown-vs-spread conflict: one camp ranks the disruption from shutdowns above the disruption from spread, the other the reverse). Split the diagnostic:
- **Floor-capacity valence (prior-independent).** Does a camp's prescription preserve the system's capacity to detect and regulate the next spillover (surveillance, honest records, not severing the channel)? Degrading that capacity is bad under every prior, the one prior-independent valence, and the real locus of the asymmetry.
- **Floor-distribution valence (prior-dependent).** Which carrier eats the cost. Not adjudicable, belongs in the gradient sector, unadjudicated. Dressing a distributional preference as a capacity argument is the failure mode to guard (S-CV-LAYER0): "constrain gain-of-function" is capacity-relevant if it reduces manufacture-of-hazard and distribution-disguised if it is really defunding a rival institution. Which one it is is auditable by where the cut falls.

This layer enters the submitted leaf only if leaf-weighting calls for it, and only translated: "the institutions controlling the evidence also shape who counts as credible, asymmetrically, and here is the price." No Foucault and no Hodge in the judge-facing prose; raw, it trips Unmapped Vocabulary.
