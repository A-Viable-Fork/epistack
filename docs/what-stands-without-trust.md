# What Stands Without Trust
### A submission to the epistemic case study competition

### How to read this submission

This submission is two of the shapes the brief names: a critique with counterexamples, and the protocol it forces.

Start where the competition starts, with synthesis, what the best deep-research tools produce. The brief asks for work that compounds across teams, survives adversarial reading, and scales with more contributors. A synthesis is one viewpoint's reach, so on its own it provides none of these, and what the brief asks for rules out the thing everyone will build. The critique is what remains once the demands are taken seriously: knowledge has to be what stands when trust in the author is removed, because everything the brief wants is a property of claims that survive without their author. Three cases show what one viewpoint's reading smooths flat: a reified dependency in physics, a hidden prior in epidemiology, a swappable frame in nutrition.

That forces the protocol, and the protocol is a climb: if knowledge is what stands when the author is removed, something has to do the removing mechanically, and building it is where the critique leads. First the type: a knowledge kernel, a fixed core that grounds claims and lints them in CI, with two markets over it, kernels to trust and surfaces to read and write through. Then one built instance, this submission's top-down meta kernel, one schema spanning the three domains. Then the generator, which instantiates the type from a config. Then the proof the generator makes real kernels: the same four cases federated bottom-up as standalone members. Each link proves the one before it, each has an oracle, and above the built climb sits the specified frontier, the coordination fabric a large federation needs to govern its writes, designed and marked as such. The same three cases run the whole way up: the critique's counterexamples, then the instance's demonstration, then the generator's output, then the federation's members. A fourth exhibit, the lineage, shows these mechanisms already run by hand in mature institutions, and that composing them is new. A fifth exhibit, the vocabulary kernel, turns the substrate on its own terms: each word the submission uses is a declaration standing by adoption, a core term grounding in the document that defines it and a borrowed term in an external citation, checked by an oracle that fails any definition drifted from its home. A sixth exhibit, the math kernel, grounds the submission's own mathematics through the gate: the properties the code computes are claims, the finite lattice laws proved at the constitutive floor by exhaustion and the earned-grade recurrence held at the checked tier by differential testing, so the engine's own math earns its standing the way any claim does.

If you read one thing, read the critique and its three cases, because it is why the rest exists. If you then run one thing, run the protocol's oracles, each of which states the invariant it checked.

Most submissions to this competition will produce impressive, readable syntheses of the three cases. I look forward to using them. Take the best version of one: it ingests the messy, contradictory record and pulls each claim from it tied to its source; it tells one claim made in different words from two claims that only look alike; it separates rhetorical force from evidential weight, surfaces the cruxes rather than settling them by fiat, and names what the record leaves out; and it renders a tangled dispute readable in minutes, across more sources than a person can hold, with an agent checking every citation and a session another researcher can fork and rerun. That tool would be excellent. I would use it.

That tool is the most a single model can do: read the record and integrate it into a view. Its limit is structural, not a weakness: a synthesis is one viewpoint's reach, and from inside a single view, however good, you cannot separate what is the world from what is the vantage, because the synthesizer is woven through it and cannot be subtracted from within. Every consequence the brief cares about follows from that one fact. The next agent has to trust it or redo it, because the judgments inside it, this claim is grounded, that one is rhetoric, this crux decides the matter, are the synthesizer's own, offered as conclusions rather than as a structure the next agent can check and extend. Two syntheses over one question do not combine, because merging them puts a person back in the loop. Against someone who disputes a claim, a synthesis offers the synthesizer's authority in place of a floor the disputant also stands on. Cheap generation sharpens the point rather than softening it: the better and cheaper the synthesis, the clearer that even the best is still one viewpoint, and the trust it asks for is the whole product. So a synthesis is read, and then it ends, because being one viewpoint's reach is what it is. The channel from a producer to the record was never typed, and a synthesis has always fused trust into view; cheap capable generation introduces no new fusion and no new failure, it multiplies the rate at which fused-trust artifacts arrive and dresses each one in the marks that used to signal care. So the typed channel matters more now and not differently, and the influx is what makes the always-missing typing load-bearing.

## What knowledge is, among reasoners who model each other

The reason the best synthesis stops there lies in what the word knowledge has to mean once there is more than one reasoner.

The definition this submission runs on is that knowledge is the invariant left as the model is attenuated. Stated of a single claim, it is the floor the kernel grounds against. Taken to what makes a claim hold at all, it completes into the mechanism: trust in the knower is replaced by a warrant that does not require the knower, and typing is how the replacement is performed. To type a claim as a measurement is to subject it to the measurement attenuation, driving the claim's dependence on its author down toward the measurement floor. The type sets the form the attenuation takes, and there are two forms, split by whether the type self-secures.

Where a type self-secures, the attenuation completes to zero, and a mathematical proof is the pure case. A valid proof asks you to trust nothing about the prover; it asks you to check the steps. So a single proof, once reviewed, subtracts the prover entirely, because what establishes the theorem is the verification and not the prover's authority, and the prover could be anyone or no one. Subtraction is attenuation's complete form, reached only by self-securing types, and it is exactly what the grounding rule does to a claim that can be checked: the gate verifies rather than trusts, which is peer review made mechanical.

Where a type does not self-secure, the attenuation approaches a floor without reaching zero, and a measurement is the case. One physicist measuring a quantity is a declaration a reader either trusts or does not, and no single measurement removes the measurer. Convergence does the attenuating instead: across many independent measurements, dependence on any one measurer is driven down toward a floor that still rests on the measurements, and the knowledge is what survives any one of them being wrong. The safety of a collider is not any one team's finding; it is what remains standing when every team's authority is attenuated and only the structure of their agreement is left. One measurement is testimony; many, composed so that no measurer is load-bearing, are knowledge. This is the LHC case's own shape.

The same attenuation places synthesis. Knowledge is a hold, a synthesis a reach. Knowledge is the cross-viewpoint invariant, composed of syntheses and identical to none, and the established ground each new synthesis starts from, so the two are a cycle: every hold was once a reach, every reach starts from holds, and no reach is itself a hold, because a hold bears weight independent of the line that found it. A synthesis stands to knowledge as one measurement stands to the measured constant. So the critique that a synthesis ends is not that it is weak but that it is one viewpoint, and one viewpoint is not knowledge until the synthesizer is attenuated out, which a lone synthesis cannot do to itself. One exception is exact: where a claim carries its own verification, a proof, the attenuation completes from inside a single synthesis, because anyone can check the steps, so one reasoner establishes knowledge with no independent convergence, and formal domains are the case where the synthesizer self-eliminates. Everywhere else, empirical and contested, a synthesis cannot secure itself, so knowledge is the invariant across viewpoints.

Verification and convergence are two forms of the one typing-attenuation, differing because the types differ in whether they self-secure. This says what the composition is and is not: not statistical averaging of noisy reports, which is only the weaker fallback where individual checking is impossible, but the removal of trust in favor of a knower-independent warrant. A synthesis can perform neither form, and this is the sharpest statement of the critique: a synthesis composes by having a synthesizer read the declarations and write a new one, so the synthesizer is never verified and never subtracted, only trusted, and subtracting them destroys the result. The structure performs both: the gate verifies what self-secures, and composition attenuates the knower out of the rest, so the synthesis a producer emits enters as claims to be grounded and a view to be chosen among, and the individual reading becomes part of what stands without its author.

The bound is exact, because the claim is strong only if it is. This produces knowledge in the specific sense of knower-independent warranted standing, not truth. The structure does not manufacture true claims; it mechanizes the verification and convergence a scientific community already treats as where knowledge lives, so that what review and replication achieve over years becomes a property the structure carries. And knower-independence is the axis the competition's own goal names, moving past single-user artifacts toward things that travel, combine, and survive scrutiny, since a thing that survives the attenuation of its producer is exactly that.

Read the definition in operational form and two requirements fall out. Justification that lives outside the agent has to live somewhere a second agent can point at: a typed structure, the claims and the reasons between them on a shared map, each labeled by what it is and what it rests on. Justification a distrusting party can check has to be decidable without appeal to the asserter, because anything decided by authority is precisely what a party who rejects the authority cannot check: mechanical grounding, where whether a claim holds is a property of the structure, computed rather than granted, the same answer for everyone. A skeptic who grants the definition has already granted these two. They are what this submission builds, and the build state of every part is graded, component by component, in the status ledger.

## The kernel, watched at work

Here are the two requirements running, on a real claim.

In this submission's tree sits the claim that LHC collisions pose no black hole catastrophe risk: a typed record in a graph. Its support edges descend to the claim that nature has run higher-energy versions of the same experiment for billions of years, down through the measured flux of ultra-high-energy cosmic rays striking the moon and surviving neutron stars, and terminate in astronomical observation: a measurement, a floor, standing that rests on something anyone can recheck. The engine computes how far the claim's support actually descends toward its floor, from the graph alone, and a reader who distrusts every author in the record recomputes the same answer, because the answer is a property of the structure and the structure is public.

Now watch what the machinery knows while it does this. It knows the claim's type, the edges beneath it, and the category of the floor those edges reach. It has no idea the claim is about physics. Hand it a claim about early COVID case clustering and it performs the identical computation down to an epidemiological estimate. Hand it the claim that eggs are healthy and it performs the identical computation down to a declaration, because a dietary definition bottoms out in a public act of defining. The floor is domain-specific: a measurement in physics, a contested estimate in epidemiology, a declaration for a definition. The kernel is what treats any floor uniformly.

This is the moment to fix a word that has been carrying two loads. One axis is trust: the deterministic, checkable core against the fallible producers around it. A producer is anything that emits claims, and a producer is an agent in the emitting role. An agent is any entity that makes claims and models other claim-makers: a person, an organization, a model, or a pipeline of these. The core is agent-agnostic because under recursive modeling the maker of a claim is always some agent modeling others, and checking the claim rather than the agent is what makes the tangle tractable, so the core checks a claim's structure rather than the agent's nature, and the boundary is checkable-core against fallible-producer with every agent checked the same. This reclaims "agent" to its broad sense, not the narrow sense of an autonomous AI tool, though such a tool is one kind of agent. The other axis is domain: whether a store of claims is about one subject or spans several. Both have traveled under the word kernel, and that is a category error, because the trusted core is about no domain at all. There is one kernel in the trust sense, the schema, the gate, and the grounding rule, and it is domain-agnostic by construction: its job is to be the part that stays fixed regardless of what claims are about. Domain-specificity lives in the content and its floors. Domain-agnosticism lives in the kernel.

Because the core checks the claim and not the agent, any differential treatment of agents, whether agents may perform a given step, whether a model is trusted like a person or held back, is a free parameter a community configures, not an architectural given. [The Parameters Register](parameters-register.md) draws that line, placing agent policy in the free tier, and the standing system makes a chosen policy enforceable. This states where the decision lives, not that agent behavior is unconstrained: the architecture makes agent policy explicit and enforceable rather than baking one in, which is safer.

The agnosticism is the pivot of everything that follows. A core that knows nothing about domains can carry more than one arrangement of domains above it, and the possible arrangements form a small, exact taxonomy.

## Three kernels

The taxonomy runs along one axis: the direction of authority over the schema.

A **domain-specific kernel** is content over the core for a single subject, with its own schema and its own floors. It is the base unit; the other two types are ways of arranging several of them.

A **bottom-up meta kernel** is assembled from pre-existing, independent domain kernels. Each keeps its own schema. They join at one shared type, and a composite selects which kernels to include for a given question. Standards emerge upward from use, and authority lives in the domain kernels: the composite proposes, the members dispose.

A **top-down meta kernel** starts from one schema designed to span the domains and instantiates them as regions within it. Authority lives in the single schema, and composition is native, because everything was one type system from the start. Under bottom-up, the LHC analysis is its own physics kernel joined after the fact to an independently built epidemiology kernel, each owning its types; under top-down, it is a region of a larger tree, and a claim in it composes with an epidemiological claim by the same rule that composes two physics claims.

The hinge that makes this an axis rather than three separate boxes is the **untyped type**. It is one type every schema shares. It holds two things and treats them identically: a claim imported from another kernel, whose foreign type the local schema does not understand, and a claim a local author declined to type. Toward both the stance is the same, this claim exists and nothing more, and it is severe in one exact way: the untyped type is not a floor, so nothing grounds through it, and a claim resting on an untyped claim inherits untyped status. That inheritance closes the laundering channel. An untyped claim is a claim no attenuation has touched, so standing cannot be imported, only earned, and it is earned by forking, at two scales: a **retail fork** casts a single untyped claim into a local type and signs the cast, one author subjecting the claim to that type's attenuation and owning the assertion; a **wholesale fork** extends the schema itself, so a whole category of crossings types natively from then on. Retail forks accumulating into a wholesale one is how a shared schema emerges from use rather than by decree: enough authors carrying the same kind of claim across the same bridge is the evidence that the bridge should be a road.

The untyped type also shows why the axis is continuous. A top-down kernel is the point where every internal crossing is an identity, one schema, no bridges; a bottom-up federation is the point where crossings are lossy untyped bridges, every transfer starting from zero standing; between them lies every intermediate state, and each end converges toward the other, federated schemas merging as wholesale forks accumulate, a single schema relaxing as regions fork away. Neither end is superior. Top-down buys native composition and coherence at the cost of one schema everyone must accept, which suits a single author or team spanning a wide but unified analysis. Bottom-up buys federation and incremental adoption at the cost of lossy crossings and weaker transferred standing, which suits a diverse community that will never agree on types. The axis is a choice of regime, and the honest move is to name which regime a given artifact was built for.

## What was built: a knowledge kernel, arranged top-down

The contribution is the knowledge kernel type: a fixed core that declares grounding, a linter that checks the declared grades on every change, and shared subtrees others opt into, opening the two markets named above. The top-down meta kernel is the instance built here, chosen for a stated reason: a sole author spanning three domains under a competition asking for one checkable analysis fits the top-down setting, where one schema is affordable and native composition is worth its price, and the same type arranged bottom-up is what a different entrant, many labs federating knowledge bases they already keep, would build instead. The instance is one designed schema, demonstrably spanning three domains that share nothing in their subject matter, physics measurement, epidemiological contestation, normative weighing. Demonstrably is the word doing the work: a schema for all things would be an imperial and unfalsifiable claim; a schema for these three, with the tree in the repository, is a checkable one.

The schema is small. Every claim sits at a position in one ordering, from the untyped type at the bottom, through the tiers of a still-contested claim, to three floors at the top: proof, measurement, and declaration. The ordering measures a single quantity, how much a downstream claim may rely on this one. Two positions ride on each claim, the highest grounding its kind could ever reach and the grounding it actually achieves given what it rests on; a claim is as settled as its kind allows exactly when the two meet, and the distance between them is the work that remains. A claim also carries its history, its origin, the crossings it has made between regions, and the forks that retyped it, so its standing is a traceable chain back to a floor rather than a bare verdict.

Grounding propagates by one rule. A claim supported by several necessary parts is as grounded as its weakest necessary part; a claim with several sufficient paths takes its strongest; support that leans on a contested assumption carries the contested value forward. In the LHC region this rule is visible doing physics: the safety argument offers several sufficient paths, cosmic ray survival of the moon, of neutron stars, of white dwarfs, and the claim takes the strongest, while any path that quietly routes through a shared assumption carries that assumption's standing up through it. The gate admits a contribution only when the grounding it declares is covered by the grounding it can show, so the graph holds nothing that claims more standing than it has earned. The floor is the sole anchor, every claim above it inherits its standing from what it rests on, never from who asserted it, and the rule is domain-blind: the arithmetic that prices a physics assumption prices an epidemiological prior. The gate checks the typing a claim carries and does not manufacture typing for raw input: a claim earns standing by being typed by an agent who stakes their own standing on it, and the gate confirms that typing holds rather than inventing it. Untyped input is admitted at the floor and grounds nothing until an agent types it and owns the typing, so the bottom of the ordering stays open while nothing grounded rests on it. That agent is any agent, a person, an organization, a model, or a pipeline of these, and the gate never inspects which, so the identical admission falls on all of them.

Around the rule sit three disciplines. The gate: reading the graph is open to everyone, and adding to it passes one check, that a contribution holds together with what is already there, so two uncoordinated contributors' output composes by machine, and a reader can run that composition in about two minutes and watch the check. The exclusion record: a refuted claim stays in the record with the reason it fell, so refutation compounds and a dead claim meets its own obituary on any attempt at revival. The periphery: the interface layer around the kernel, a plain-language walk, a terse specialist view, an inspect-and-dependents console, a side-by-side dispute view, a control that flips an assumption and shows the conclusion give way, all of it reading through one contract, all of it thickened only where the demonstration needs it, the smallest surface that shows what a checkable kernel enables.

The proof that the top-down kernel does real epistemic work is the three cases, and each yields the counterexample a good synthesis would have smoothed flat.

The LHC safety argument looks robust: several independent lines of evidence converging on safety. It looks that way until the graph reifies the dependency the lines share as a node, and the convergence reprices as one assumption wearing several coats. In the tree this is a single operation, the shared node made explicit and the contamination rule doing the rest; in prose it is a subtlety that survives only as long as the author remembers to mention it.

The COVID origins evidence looks decisive, in whichever direction the teller favors. It looks that way until the prior each verdict turns on is named as a claim of its own, and the debate resolves into a disagreement over one weighing, held in the open where a reader can reprice it. The engine traces the loudest statistical dispute to a method pushed past the range it was built for, prices what each side assumes, and leaves the verdict where it belongs, with the reader.

The egg nutrition verdict looks like a fact. It looks that way until the frame it presupposes, which population, which endpoint, which food it replaces, is swapped and the verdict swaps with it. The engine splits the casual question into the several it contains, answers the parts a measurement settles, and carries the definitional part down to its floor, a declaration, which is where a claim about what counts as healthy honestly terminates.

Three domains, three floors, three counterexamples, one schema. That sentence is the demonstration: the agent that drew each case made the shared node explicit, a reified dependency in physics, a hidden prior in epidemiology, a swappable frame in nutrition, and the same grounding arithmetic reprices each one because the kernel never knew the difference, so a reader can recompute the collapse without trusting the agent that drew it.

The submission's second job follows from the first. A top-down kernel whose schema is public is a base others can fork, retail per crossing and wholesale per category, and the untyped type already sits at the bottom of this tree's ordering, so the artifact demonstrates the top-down type and simultaneously ships the hinge the other direction needs.

### The climb, built and checked

The claim that the contribution is the type, not the instance, is a checked fact, because the type is carried up four steps and each step has an oracle. The type is the knowledge kernel, built here as the top-down instance and demonstrated on the three cases above. The content-addressed crossing is next: a type is a bundle whose hash freezes its meaning, so two kernels that mean the same thing by a type pin the same hash, and `build/check-crossing.mjs` proves a same-hash crossing composes native and lossless while an unpinned crossing arrives untyped and grounds nothing until a fork restores standing. `build/check-agreement.mjs` then verifies the four cases agree four-way on the common kinds, which is what lets those kinds be published as a shared subtree. The generator, `scaffolder/new-kernel.mjs`, instantiates a kernel from a config and runs the real gate and the real check over its output, proven by regenerating a case's schema from config with the tables reproduced exactly. And the proof the generated kernels are real is the bottom-up federation: `build/check-bottomup.mjs` runs the same four cases as standalone members and confirms adversarially that each owns its schema, that standing crosses only through an owned fork so no member's authority reaches another, and that same-hash crossings compose native. The generator regenerating the cases and the cases federating as standalone members is what turns "the contribution is the type" from a sentence into a checked property. The protocol this climb builds is stated normatively and standalone in [The Protocol Specification](protocol-spec.md), the record formats, the grounding computation, the gate, and the crossing extracted so a team can implement a conforming kernel without reading this argument. Above this built climb sits the specified frontier, the coordination fabric that governs writes to a federation at scale, named in the status ledger and marked as such. That open form, a built core with a specified frontier named on purpose, is not incompleteness but the shape a moving target forces on a knowledge system whose subjects model it back, so the transparency mechanisms, the built-versus-specified line, the characterized gaps, the ledgers, are load-bearing structure rather than disclosure; the argument for why is `docs/on-transparency.md`.

## Where it points

The bottom-up meta kernel is built, the four cases federated as standalone members and verified as the climb's last step, and it is the arrangement for the other regime, many authors who will never share a schema. Independent kernels, each owning its own types, join at the untyped type; composites select members per question; standing crosses only by owned forks; and retail forks accumulate into wholesale ones, so a federation grows a shared vocabulary out of use, the way a path becomes a road because people kept walking it. The mechanics, the composite, the transfer semantics, and the standards-from-use dynamics are detailed in the appendix and run by the bottom-up build. [The Parameters Register](parameters-register.md) draws the governance line between what a community sets locally and what every kernel must hold to stay composable: a free tier a community configures, time locks, standing rules, agent policy, type system, and a thin required tier of composition invariants no kernel may break without leaving the protocol. Diversity in the free tier, unity in the required tier, is the taxonomy seen as governance, and it is what makes the configurability concrete.

Where this goes past the built work, the continuation and why the next phase is worth funding, is set out in [Where This Goes](vision-and-continuation.md): the destination, the roadmap that begins at a present problem, and why the commercial path aligns with the commons rather than capturing it.

## A note on evaluating an enabler

There is a tension in assessing this submission, and it is more honest to name it than to let it resolve by default. Uplift runs on two ordered axes. One is teaching: bringing a reader up to speed, which strong synthesis tools do well. The other is claim: improving the knowledge itself as a checkable, composable object that survives the removal of trust. Teaching is downstream of claim, because a view stands on a trust-side object and only the claim axis makes such objects: a better synthesis teaches better and makes nothing more checkable, while a checkable claim teaches the shape a synthesis buries and seeds a market of teachers over one trust. This submission contributes on the claim axis, the upstream one, and is deliberately modest on its own direct teaching, because the contribution is the trust object the teaching axis composes over.

On the claim axis, the uplift has a present component, what the artifact does directly, grounding a claim's standing checkably, making refutations stick, tracing provenance, locating a disagreement's frontier; and an enabled component, larger, which is why the separation of trust from view matters: because trust is established once and shared, a market of interfaces can form over it, each improving the claim axis for a different reader, none of them having to be trusted about the grounding, claim uplift compounded across many peripheries rather than delivered by one.

The same allocation places automated ingestion, on the producer side of the market rather than the consumer side. The two layers the influx makes necessary are the gate that checks the typing of what arrives and the store that composes and keeps it, and both are built here; ingestion, the layer that reads a messy record and emits candidate claims, is the producer layer the market supplies and sharpens as generation improves, kept thin here on purpose because it is the layer the influx is already good at. So a strong automated-ingestion entry elsewhere in this competition is a producer this protocol would gate like any other, a complementary contribution it would compound rather than a gap in this one. This is the division of labor the gate defines and not a capability the kernel lacks: typing a claim is the claimant's work, so a thin ingestion connector is the correct allocation rather than a missing feature.

The API boundary is open to any producer on the same terms. A synthesis-tool builder plugs in by emitting typed claims with their support through the one contract and receiving an accept-with-standing or the reason it was held back, never touching the store and never trusted about the grounding. So the submission is infrastructure the ecosystem's tools compose into rather than a competitor to any of them, and the better the producers that arrive, the more the channel holds. The worked demonstration that the boundary is two-sided and general is the workflow atlas: producers of any source shape, a spoken debate, a live news page, a clean dataset, converge at the one built gate, consumers of any question shape diverge from the one built read contract, and an AI is one producer and one consumer among them (`docs/workflow-atlas.md`).

The enabled component is where the scoping choice lives: evaluate the artifact alone, or the architecture as an enabler. A submission whose contribution is an enabler delivers its value in what it enables: the compounding and scaling the competition names are properties of the market that forms over the trust object, not of the artifact standing alone. The bound is the one the whole submission keeps: the market is enabled, not guaranteed, so the claim is to the enablement, which the external contract's feasibility demonstrates, not to the realized uplift of interfaces not yet built. Whether to credit enablement is the evaluator's to decide; naming it is a submission about making structure explicit making the structure of its own evaluation explicit too.

## Appendix: the federation, and standards from use

The main argument locates the bottom-up meta kernel as built, the four cases federated as standalone members and verified by `build/check-bottomup.mjs`. This appendix documents the federation the bottom-up build runs, and states its trade-offs as design rather than as deficiency.

**The members.** A federation begins with domain kernels that already exist and were built without coordination: a physics kernel with proof and measurement floors, an epidemiology kernel whose floors are estimates with stated methods, a policy kernel whose floors are declarations. Each is standalone, its schema answers to its own authors, and nothing in the federation can change a member's types from outside. That independence is what lets a community adopt the protocol without first agreeing with anyone.

**The composite.** A composite is a selection, a named set of member kernels assembled for a question, together with the crossings its author has built between them. The composite is itself a kernel in the trust sense, its selection and crossings are typed records behind a gate, and it carries no authority over its members. Two composites over overlapping members can disagree about which crossings to make, and both stand; the disagreement is itself recorded structure.

**Transfer semantics.** A claim entering a composite from a member arrives as the untyped type. Nothing grounds through it, and everything resting on it is untyped in turn. This deliberately weak default is the honest price of federation: transferred standing starts at zero because the receiving schema has no basis for anything more. The alternative, automatic translation between schemas, would require the protocol to understand every member's type system, which reintroduces at the seam exactly the trusted authority the kernel exists to remove.

**Retail forks.** A retail fork is one author casting one untyped claim into a local type and signing the cast. The signature matters: the crossing is a claim of the author's own, with the author's standing behind it, gated and recorded like any other claim. If the source claim changes in its home kernel, the fork does not silently follow; it points at a version, and staleness is a checkable fact about the crossing. Retail forks are cheap, local, and reversible, which is why they are the unit of adoption.

**Wholesale forks.** A wholesale fork extends a schema so a category of crossings types natively. It is a heavier act, a schema change under the receiving kernel's own governance, and its justification is empirical: when many authors have independently cast the same kind of foreign claim into the same local type, the category has proven itself in use, and the wholesale fork ratifies a practice rather than legislating one. This is standards from use: the shared vocabulary of a federation is grown, crossing by crossing, out of the public record of what people actually needed to carry over, so the case for each extension is checkable like everything else.

**Convergence.** As wholesale forks accumulate, member schemas grow shared regions and the federation drifts toward the top-down end of the axis; a region of a top-down kernel whose authors want different types forks away and rejoins across an untyped bridge. The axis stays open in both directions, per the taxonomy. And the untyped type, already present at the bottom of this submission's ordering, is the single piece of machinery the two arrangements share.
