# What Stands Without Trust
### A submission to the epistemic case study competition

Most submissions to this competition will produce impressive, readable syntheses of the three cases. I look forward to using them.

Take the best version of one. It ingests the messy, contradictory record, papers and preprints and testimony and data, and pulls each claim from it tied to its source. It sees when two sources make one claim in different words and when they make two claims that only look alike, so it neither double counts nor flattens. It resolves which claims support which, separates a claim's rhetorical force from its evidential weight, surfaces the cruxes rather than settling them by fiat, and names what the record leaves out. It renders all of this so a reader takes in a tangled dispute in minutes. Give it the strongest available model and it does this across more sources than a person can hold, with an agent checking every citation and figure, and a session another researcher can fork and rerun. That tool would be excellent. I would use it.

It still stands short of knowledge. What it makes is a synthesis, organized for a reader, and the next agent that wants to build on it has to trust it or redo it. The judgments inside it, this claim is grounded, that one is rhetoric, this crux decides the matter, are the synthesizer's own, offered as conclusions rather than as a structure the next agent can check and extend. Its organization lives in prose, and prose carries argument the way a voice does, by asking to be believed. Two of these syntheses over one question do not combine; merging them puts a person back in the loop. Against someone who disputes a claim, a synthesis offers the synthesizer's authority in place of a floor the disputant also has to stand on. Cheap generation finishes the clearing: when a plausible synthesis costs nothing to produce, the trust it asks for is the entire product, and that trust now hangs on air. So a synthesis is read, and then it ends. However good, it is the single-user artifact this competition names as the thing that fails to travel, combine, or survive scrutiny.

## What knowledge is, among reasoners who model each other

The reason the best synthesis stops there lies in what the word knowledge has to mean once there is more than one reasoner.

The definition this submission runs on is that knowledge is what stands when trust in the knower is subtracted. Stated of a single claim, it is the floor the kernel grounds against. Taken to what makes a claim hold at all, it completes into an account of how the subtraction is actually performed, and the completion is that trust in the knower is replaced by a warrant that does not require the knower. There are two such warrants, and which applies depends on whether a single instance of the claim can be checked.

Where a claim is checkable, the knower is subtracted by verification, and a mathematical proof is the pure case. A valid proof asks you to trust nothing about the prover; it asks you to check the steps. So a single proof, once reviewed, removes the prover entirely, because what establishes the theorem is the verification and not the prover's authority, and the prover could be anyone or no one. This is the strongest form of the subtraction, complete in one step, and it is exactly what the grounding rule does to a claim that can be checked: the gate verifies rather than trusts, which is peer review made mechanical. A verified proof is the cleanest instance of the whole thesis, a claim from which trust has been removed entirely and replaced by a check.

Where a claim cannot be checked in a single instance, the knower is subtracted by independent convergence, and a measurement is the case. One physicist measuring a quantity is a declaration a reader either trusts or does not, and no single measurement removes the measurer. The measurer is subtracted instead across many independent measurements, and the knowledge is what survives any one of them being wrong. The safety of a collider is not any one team's finding; it is what remains standing when every team's authority is subtracted and only the structure of their agreement is left. One measurement is testimony; many, composed so that no measurer is load-bearing, are knowledge. This is the LHC case's own shape: its safety is known not because any single argument or measurement is trusted, but because what survives the composition holds independent of any of them.

These are two ways to do one thing, replace trust in the knower with a warrant that does not need the knower, verification where a check is available and convergence where it is not. This matters because it says what the composition is and is not. It is not statistical averaging of noisy reports, which is only the weaker fallback used where individual checking is impossible; it is the removal of trust in favor of a knower-independent warrant. A synthesis can perform neither warrant, and this is the sharpest form of the critique of synthesis: a synthesis composes by having a synthesizer read the declarations and write a new one, so the synthesizer is never verified and never subtracted, only trusted, and subtracting them destroys the result. The structure performs both: the gate verifies what can be checked, composition subtracts the knower from the rest, and what emerges is held by the structure rather than by anyone's word.

The bound is precise, because the claim is strong only if it is exact. This produces knowledge in the specific sense of knower-independent warranted standing, not truth. The structure does not manufacture true claims; it mechanizes the verification and convergence that a scientific community already treats as where knowledge lives, so that what review and replication achieve over years becomes a property the structure carries. Knower-independence is the output, not truth. And this is the axis the competition's own goal names, moving past single-user artifacts toward things that travel, combine, and survive scrutiny, since a thing that survives the subtraction of its producer is exactly a thing whose knowledge is knower-independent.

Read the definition in operational form and two requirements fall out, one from each half. Justification that lives outside the agent has to live somewhere, as objects a second agent can point at: a typed structure, the claims and the reasons between them on a shared map, each labeled by what it is and what it rests on. Justification a distrusting party can check has to be decidable without appeal to the asserter, because anything decided by authority is precisely what a party who rejects the authority cannot check: mechanical grounding, where whether a claim holds is a property of the structure, computed rather than granted, the same answer for everyone. A skeptic who grants the definition has already granted these two. They are what this submission builds, and the build state of every part named below is graded, component by component, in the status ledger.

## The kernel, watched at work

Here are the two requirements running, on a real claim.

In this submission's tree sits the claim that LHC collisions pose no black hole catastrophe risk. It is a typed record in a graph. Its support edges descend to the claim that nature has run higher-energy versions of the same experiment for billions of years, which descends through the measured flux of ultra-high-energy cosmic rays striking the moon and surviving neutron stars, and terminates in astronomical observation: a measurement, which is a floor, a place where a claim's standing rests on something anyone can recheck. The engine computes how far the claim's support actually descends toward its floor, and it computes this from the graph alone. A reader who distrusts every author in the record recomputes the same answer, because the answer is a property of the structure, and the structure is public.

Now watch what the machinery knows while it does this. It knows the claim's type, the edges beneath it, and the category of the floor those edges reach. It has no idea the claim is about physics. Hand the same machinery a claim about early COVID case clustering and it performs the identical computation down to an epidemiological estimate. Hand it the claim that eggs are healthy and it performs the identical computation down to a declaration, because a dietary definition bottoms out in a public act of defining. The floor is domain-specific: a measurement in physics, a contested estimate in epidemiology, a declaration for a definition. The kernel is what treats any floor uniformly.

This is the moment to fix a word that has been carrying two loads. One axis is trust: the deterministic, checkable core against the fallible producers around it. A producer is anything that emits claims, a person, a model, or an agentic pipeline, and the core checks a claim's structure rather than the claimant's nature, so the boundary is checkable-core against fallible-producer and the core checks all producers the same. The other axis is domain: whether a store of claims is about one subject or spans several. Both have traveled under the word kernel, and that is a category error, because the trusted core is about no domain at all. There is one kernel in the trust sense, the schema, the gate, and the grounding rule, and it is domain-agnostic by construction: its job is to be the part that stays fixed regardless of what claims are about. Domain-specificity lives in the content and its floors. Domain-agnosticism lives in the kernel.

Because the core checks the claim and not the claimant, any differential treatment of producers, whether agents may perform a given step, whether a model is trusted like a person or held back, is a free parameter a community configures, not an architectural given. The register that draws that line is [The Parameters Register](parameters-register.md), which places agent policy in the free tier; the standing system is the mechanism that makes such a policy enforceable once a community sets it. This is a statement about where the decision lives, not a claim that agent behavior is unconstrained: the architecture makes agent policy explicit and enforceable rather than assuming one policy, which is safer than baking one in.

The agnosticism is the pivot of everything that follows. A core that knows nothing about domains can carry more than one arrangement of domains above it, and the possible arrangements form a small, exact taxonomy.

## Three kernels

The taxonomy runs along one axis: the direction of authority over the schema.

A **domain-specific kernel** is content over the core for a single subject, with its own schema and its own floors. It is the base unit; the other two types are ways of arranging several of them.

A **bottom-up meta kernel** is assembled from pre-existing, independent domain kernels. Each keeps its own schema. They join at one shared type, and a composite selects which kernels to include for a given question. Standards emerge upward from use, and authority lives in the domain kernels: the composite proposes, the members dispose. The LHC analysis under this arrangement would be its own kernel with a physics schema, joined after the fact to an independently built epidemiology kernel, each sovereign over its own types.

A **top-down meta kernel** starts from one schema designed to span the domains and instantiates them as regions within it. Authority lives in the single schema, and composition is native, because everything was one type system from the start. The LHC analysis under this arrangement is a region of a larger tree, and a claim in it composes with an epidemiological claim by the same rule that composes two physics claims, since to the kernel they were always the same kind of object.

The hinge that makes this an axis rather than three separate boxes is the **untyped type**. It is one type every schema shares. It holds two things and treats them identically: a claim imported from another kernel, whose foreign type the local schema does not understand, and a claim a local author declined to type. The protocol's stance toward both is the same, this claim exists and nothing more is warranted, and the treatment is severe in one exact way: the untyped type is not a floor, so nothing grounds through it. A claim resting on an untyped claim inherits untyped status. That inheritance closes the laundering channel; standing cannot be imported, only earned, and the composition stays honest without the protocol understanding any foreign type system.

Standing is earned out of the untyped type by forking, at two scales. A **retail fork** takes a single untyped claim, casts it into a local type, and signs the crossing: one author asserting that this foreign object is, in the local schema, a measurement or an estimate or a declaration, and owning that assertion as a claim of their own. A **wholesale fork** takes the kernel itself and extends its schema, so that a whole category of crossings types natively from then on. Retail forks accumulating into a wholesale one is how a shared schema emerges from use rather than by decree: enough authors carrying the same kind of claim across the same bridge is the evidence that the bridge should be a road.

The untyped type also shows why the axis is continuous. A top-down kernel is the point where every internal crossing is an identity, one schema, no bridges. A bottom-up federation is the point where crossings are lossy untyped bridges, every transfer starting from zero standing. Between them lies every intermediate state, and each end converges toward the other: federated schemas merge as wholesale forks accumulate, and a single schema relaxes as regions fork away. Neither end is superior. Top-down buys native composition and coherence at the cost of one schema everyone must accept, which suits a single author or team spanning a wide but unified analysis. Bottom-up buys federation and incremental adoption at the cost of lossy crossings and weaker transferred standing, which suits a diverse community that will never agree on types. The axis is a choice of regime, and the honest move is to name which regime a given artifact was built for.

## What was built: a top-down meta kernel

This submission is a top-down meta kernel, and that is its exact and stronger description: one designed schema, demonstrably spanning three domains that share nothing in their subject matter, physics measurement, epidemiological contestation, normative weighing. Demonstrably is the word doing the work. A schema for all things would be an imperial and unfalsifiable claim; a schema for these three, with the tree in the repository, is a checkable one.

The schema is small. Every claim sits at a position in one ordering, from the untyped type at the bottom, through the tiers of a still-contested claim, to three floors at the top: proof, measurement, and declaration. The ordering measures a single quantity, how much a downstream claim may rely on this one. Two positions ride on each claim, the highest grounding its kind could ever reach and the grounding it actually achieves given what it rests on; a claim is as settled as its kind allows exactly when the two meet, and the distance between them is the work that remains. A claim also carries its history as part of what it holds: its origin, the crossings it has made between regions, and the forks that retyped it are recorded on the claim, so its standing is a traceable chain back to a floor rather than a bare verdict, and any reader can walk that chain.

Grounding propagates by one rule. A claim supported by several necessary parts is as grounded as its weakest necessary part; a claim with several sufficient paths takes its strongest; support that leans on a contested assumption carries the contested value forward. In the LHC region this rule is visible doing physics: the safety argument offers several sufficient paths, cosmic ray survival of the moon, of neutron stars, of white dwarfs, and the claim takes the strongest, while any path that quietly routes through a shared assumption finds that assumption's standing carried up through it. The gate admits a contribution only when the grounding it declares is covered by the grounding it can show, so the graph holds nothing that claims more standing than it has earned. The floor is the sole anchor, and every claim above it inherits its standing by this rule from what it rests on, never from who asserted it. This is the checkable-by-a-distrusting-party clause made precise, and it is domain-blind: the rule that prices a physics assumption prices an epidemiological prior by the same arithmetic.

Around the rule sit three disciplines. The gate: reading the graph is open to everyone, and adding to it passes one check, that a contribution holds together with what is already there; two uncoordinated contributors' output composes by machine, and a reader can run that composition in about two minutes and watch the check. The exclusion record: a refuted claim stays in the record with the reason it fell, so refutation compounds and a dead claim meets its own obituary on any attempt at revival. The periphery: the interface layer around the kernel, a plain-language walk, a terse specialist view, an inspect-and-dependents console, a side-by-side dispute view, a control that flips an assumption and shows the conclusion give way, all of it reading through one contract and leaving the stored truth to the gate, and all of it kept deliberately thin, the smallest surface that shows what a checkable kernel enables.

The proof that the top-down kernel does real epistemic work is the three cases, and each yields the counterexample a good synthesis would have smoothed flat.

The LHC safety argument looks robust: several independent lines of evidence converging on safety. It looks that way until the graph reifies the dependency the lines share as a node, and the convergence reprices as one assumption wearing several coats. In the tree this is a single operation, the shared node made explicit and the contamination rule doing the rest; in prose it is a subtlety that survives only as long as the author remembers to mention it.

The COVID origins evidence looks decisive, in whichever direction the teller favors. It looks that way until the prior each verdict turns on is named as a claim of its own, and the debate resolves into a disagreement over one weighing, held in the open where a reader can reprice it. The engine traces the loudest statistical dispute to a method pushed past the range it was built for, prices what each side assumes, and leaves the verdict where it belongs, with the reader.

The egg nutrition verdict looks like a fact. It looks that way until the frame it presupposes, which population, which endpoint, which food it replaces, is swapped and the verdict swaps with it. The engine splits the casual question into the several it contains, answers the parts a measurement settles, and carries the definitional part down to its floor, a declaration, which is where a claim about what counts as healthy honestly terminates.

Three domains, three floors, three counterexamples, one schema. That single sentence is the demonstration: the same grounding arithmetic exposed a reified dependency in physics, a hidden prior in epidemiology, and a swappable frame in nutrition, and it could do so because the kernel never knew the difference.

The submission's second job follows from the first. A top-down kernel whose schema is public is a base schema others can fork: retail, per crossing, casting a single foreign claim into these types and owning the cast; wholesale, per category, extending the schema so a new kind of claim types natively. The untyped type already sits at the bottom of the ordering in this tree. The artifact demonstrates the top-down type and simultaneously ships the hinge the other direction needs.

## Where it points

The bottom-up meta kernel is the future work this artifact already enables, and it is a design for a different regime, many authors who will never share a schema. Independent kernels, each sovereign over its own types, joining at the untyped type; composites selecting members per question; standing crossing only by owned forks; retail forks accumulating into wholesale ones, so a federation grows a shared vocabulary out of use, the way a path becomes a road because people kept walking it. The mechanics, the composite, the transfer semantics, and the standards-from-use dynamics are given in the appendix. What draws the line between what a community sets locally and what every kernel must hold to stay composable is [The Parameters Register](parameters-register.md): a free tier a community configures to its own situation, time locks, standing rules, agent policy, type system, and a thin required tier of composition invariants no kernel may break without leaving the protocol. Diversity in the free tier, unity in the required tier, is the same taxonomy seen as governance, and it is what makes the configurability concrete rather than gestured at. The submission built the right thing for one author spanning three domains, and it names, with its hinge already in the tree, the thing to build for many.

## A note on evaluating an enabler

There is a tension in assessing this submission, and it is more honest to name it than to let it resolve by default. Uplift runs on two orthogonal axes. One is teaching: bringing a reader up to speed, which strong synthesis tools do well. The other is claim: improving the knowledge itself as a checkable, composable object that survives the removal of trust. This submission contributes on the second axis and is deliberately modest on the first, which means an assessment calibrated to teaching uplift will see little and an assessment calibrated to claim uplift will see the contribution.

On the claim axis, the submission's uplift has a present component and an enabled one. The present component is what the artifact does directly: grounding a claim's standing checkably, making refutations stick, tracing provenance, locating a disagreement's frontier. The enabled component is larger and it is the reason the separation of trust from view matters: because trust is established once and shared, a market of interfaces can form over it, each improving the claim axis for a different reader, none of them having to be trusted about the grounding. That is claim uplift compounded across many peripheries rather than delivered by one.

The enabled component is where the scoping choice lives, and the choice is whether to evaluate the artifact alone or the architecture as an enabler. A submission whose contribution is an enabler is, by the competition's own compounding and scaling criteria, meant to be evaluated on what it enables, so evaluating the artifact in isolation would apply those criteria incompletely. The bound is the one the whole submission keeps: the market is enabled, not guaranteed, so the claim is not to the realized uplift of interfaces not yet built, but to the enablement, which the external contract's feasibility demonstrates. Whether to credit enablement is the evaluator's to decide; we name it because it materially changes the assessment and because a submission about making structure explicit should make this structure of its own evaluation explicit too.

## Postscript: a dated note on scaling (late June 2026)

*A dated observation, added after the argument above, which stands on its own.*

In late June 2026, during the competition window, a new AI workbench for scientific research, Claude Science, was released: a working environment with artifact generation, a reviewer agent, session forking, and integrated tools and connectors. It does not interoperate with this kernel, and the note does not claim it does. The point is structural: a purpose-built science workbench occupies exactly the periphery slot this architecture predicts. Its generative and reviewing work is producer-and-consumer work, and its own design already has that work enter a store through a gate and be read back through a contract; a core that stays fixed while the surface around it changes is the shape it too converges on. So the discipline is the same whether the producer is a person, a general model, or a purpose-built workbench: the periphery is where capability accrues, and the membrane is what holds. This is the scaling criterion shown by an event in the world rather than argued on paper: as the tools improve, the periphery improves, and the kernel does not move. The kernel's domain-agnosticism is the same property seen from another side; a core that is indifferent to what claims are about is equally indifferent to what produces them. Dated as a structural observation, not a claim about the product's current features, so it does not decay as those features change.

## Appendix: the federation, and standards from use

The main argument locates the bottom-up meta kernel as future work. This appendix specifies it far enough that a builder could start, and far enough that its trade-offs read as design rather than as deficiency.

**The members.** A federation begins with domain kernels that already exist and were built without coordination: a physics kernel with proof and measurement floors, an epidemiology kernel whose floors are estimates with stated methods, a policy kernel whose floors are declarations. Each is sovereign. Its schema answers to its own authors, and nothing in the federation can change a member's types from outside. This sovereignty is the point of the arrangement; it is what lets a community adopt the protocol without first agreeing with anyone.

**The composite.** A composite is a selection, a named set of member kernels assembled for a question, together with the crossings its author has built between them. The composite is itself a kernel in the trust sense, its selection and crossings are typed records behind a gate, and it carries no authority over its members. Two composites over overlapping members can disagree about which crossings to make, and both stand; the disagreement is itself recorded structure.

**Transfer semantics.** A claim entering a composite from a member arrives as the untyped type. Nothing grounds through it, and everything resting on it is untyped in turn. This is the deliberately weak default, and its weakness is the honest price of federation: transferred standing starts at zero because the receiving schema has no basis for anything more. The alternative, automatic translation between schemas, would require the protocol to understand every member's type system, which reintroduces at the seam exactly the trusted authority the kernel exists to remove.

**Retail forks.** A retail fork is one author casting one untyped claim into a local type and signing the cast. The signature matters: the crossing is a claim of the author's own, with the author's standing behind it, gated and recorded like any other claim. If the source claim changes in its home kernel, the fork does not silently follow; it points at a version, and staleness is a checkable fact about the crossing. Retail forks are cheap, local, and reversible, which is why they are the unit of adoption.

**Wholesale forks.** A wholesale fork extends a schema so a category of crossings types natively. It is a heavier act, a schema change under the receiving kernel's own governance, and its justification is empirical: the accumulated retail forks are the evidence. When many authors have independently cast the same kind of foreign claim into the same local type, the category has proven itself in use, and the wholesale fork ratifies a practice rather than legislating one. This is standards from use: the shared vocabulary of a federation is grown, crossing by crossing, out of the record of what people actually needed to carry over, and the record is public, so the case for each extension is checkable like everything else.

**Convergence.** As wholesale forks accumulate, member schemas grow shared regions, and the federation drifts toward the top-down end of the axis, native composition over an ever larger common vocabulary. The drift can also run the other way; a region of a top-down kernel whose authors want different types forks away and rejoins the federation across an untyped bridge. The axis stays open in both directions, which is what makes it an axis.

**The regime distinction, restated.** The top-down kernel this submission builds suits its regime: one author, one span of analysis, coherence and native composition worth the price of a single schema. The federation suits the other regime: many authors, no shared schema now or ever, adoption that must be incremental and sovereignty that must be respected, worth the price of lossy crossings and standing that transfers only when someone owns the transfer. Both are arrangements of domains over the same domain-agnostic kernel, and the untyped type, already present at the bottom of this submission's ordering, is the single piece of machinery the two arrangements share.
