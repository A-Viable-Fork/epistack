# Knowledge Kernels and Crypto: the DOs and DON'Ts

Decentralized finance is the adjacent field that tested our mechanisms under sustained adversarial attack at scale, and its lineage entries in this repository read its history honestly: it anticipated provable-fault slashing, light-client verification, time-locks, and commit-reveal, and it stopped short, each time, by coupling the consequence to a transferable token, so credibility stayed purchasable. This document is the other direction of that exchange: what a market layer over a knowledge kernel may do, what it may not, and why most of the may-nots are theorems rather than rules.

## The three layers

**The knowledge layer** is the kernel: typed claims, the gate, computed standing, content-addressed and forkable, free to read forever. **The market layer** is everything financial that references the knowledge layer: access metering, prediction markets, insurance, adversarial bounties. It may reference grades; it may never move them. **The excluded layer** is anything that would let money move a grade or enclose the graph: tradable standing, leveraged derivatives on reputation, exclusive licenses to claims, market-influenced gates. The first layer is the protocol's. The second is open to anyone. The third does not survive contact with the architecture, and the point of this document is to show which exclusions are enforced by the world rather than by rules.

The thin shared root, stated once so nothing else in this document masquerades as it: **reading and recomputing are free, always** (anyone fetches, verifies, and re-derives every grade; no community parameter can price this, because it is what makes trust unownable); **standing moves only through typing acts** (money references grades and never moves one); **slashing triggers on provable fault only.** Everything else is a free parameter. A community may require stake to claim, stake to attest, identity grades to act, or none of these; the split that keeps it coherent is that verification-as-recompute is free even in the most stake-gated community, while verification-as-attestation is an act, and acts are priceable. Clients may integrate market machinery or refuse it as their own identity; both are legitimate citizens. Stake-gated and stake-free communities cross exactly as strangers already do, because admission friction never enters grade semantics and every community's claims carry its governance-hash.

## The plural-bridge theorem, first, because it dissolves half the document

A bridge is any service connecting the graph to on-chain mechanisms: metered APIs, snapshot anchoring, market oracles, treasury contracts. The one architectural law is the same one that governs clients: **no bridge holds a capability any operator with the snapshot lacks.** The graph is public, snapshots are static files, grades recompute on any device, and light-client verification (the field's own gift, generalized: a Merkle path against a posted snapshot hash) lets a contract check inclusion without trusting an attestor. Therefore bridges are plural by construction, and the consequences arrive as theorems:

- **Reading cannot be tolled.** A bridge that charges individuals to read is routed around by the free graph and by every competing operator. No subsidy program is needed to protect free reading; the architecture already gave it away permanently. What a bridge sells is convenience: throughput, SLAs, uptime, integration. Selling convenience over a free commons is honest business; selling the commons is impossible.
- **The graph cannot be enclosed.** No exclusive license to a claim, subgraph, or contributor's work is sellable, because the thing licensed is a hash anyone holds. Institutional licensing is therefore metered, non-exclusive, non-proprietary by construction, and a corporation pays for scale and guarantees, never for ownership.
- **There is no governance to buy.** The protocol has no token, no treasury, no parameter a market position can vote on. A bridge's own infrastructure parameters are the bridge operator's business; kernel content, grades, and typing are a community's, reachable only through the gate and the community's own typing acts.
- **No sunset clause is needed.** A singular bridge must promise to dissolve itself; plural bridges die by competition. The absence of the promise is the stronger guarantee.

## DOs

**DO keep the kernel free, content-addressed, and forkable, with the market layer as a reference-only consumer.** If every bridge contract is exploited simultaneously, the graph must remain exactly as readable, forkable, and verifiable as before. Design for that day.

**DO bind every market instrument to (claim identity, governance-hash).** "Will this claim reach checked" is ill-posed; checked under whose parameters? A community's governance-hash freezes its parameterization, so a market referencing (claim, governance-hash) settles against a recomputable fact, and the same claim under two communities is honestly two markets. Settlement is by recompute against the published snapshot at the settlement time, optimistically posted and challengeable by anyone who recomputes differently within the window; the oracle is a verification, never an attestation, and where a genuine attestation of off-graph fact is needed, that is the oracle problem, named in the lineage corpus as the open frontier it is, not assumed solved here.

**DO let markets do the co-circulation they are good at.** Resolution markets on grade outcomes surface confidence. Insurance on refutation risk prices epistemic exposure for institutions that build on a dependency chain. Liquidity provision serves whoever is metering access. Adversarial bounty staking pays for diligence: stake against a claim, and if the graph demotes it within the window (new evidence admitted, support undercut, grade recomputed down), the stake returns with the bounty; resolution is the recompute, never a tribunal. All of these reference grades. None touches one.

**DO reward contributors from usage, auditable against the graph.** A community or operator that runs a treasury (funded by access metering, market fees, or anything else) can distribute to contributors by reference weight, and the crucial property is free: **reference counts are recomputable from the public graph**, so every distribution is auditable by anyone, a contestable dashboard over the treasury's own books, no trusted counter anywhere. Distribution policy (decay curves that prevent early-contributor aristocracy, weighting by grade earned, cycle length) is the community's parameter, stated in its parameter record under its governance-hash, not the protocol's law. Attribution itself is structural and unstrippable: provenance is hashes.

**DO route institutional accountability through attestation, not a KYC contractor.** The identity layer already grades presented identities by their attestation structure. An operator that wants accountable institutional customers requires presentation at a grade backed by institutional attestations (a domain proof, a registry binding, a jurisdiction attestation), which is liability routing with the same machinery everything else uses, plural attestors instead of one verifier. The same move replaces the "recognized academic" exemption: exemption policies are an operator's or community's choice, evaluated against presented attestations, with no admissions office anywhere.

**DO use the field's defensive mechanics where they earned their keep.** Time-delayed distribution locks against flash-assembled positions. Duration requirements on any stake that can trigger review, so nothing reviewable is flash-borrowable. Manipulation-resistant reads (time-weighted, never spot) for any UI that displays market prices near grades. Commit-reveal for submission priority where front-running of pending contributions is a live concern. These are the lineage entries cashed in.

**DO make every financial flow a typed record.** Treasury inflows, distributions, stake movements, settlements: typed, content-addressed, graded like anything else, rendered by the contestable dashboard. The market layer is not exempt from the graph it lives on.

## DON'Ts

**DON'T tokenize standing, and understand why you can't.** Standing is not a balance; it is the output of a recomputation anyone can run. There is no object to transfer, collateralize, or lend against. A "standing token" is a claim about a public function, and a derivative on it is a derivative on arithmetic. The field's soulbound-token designs were an attempt to bolt non-transferability onto things that were balances; here non-transferability is what standing is made of.

**DON'T let any market signal move a grade.** Standing moves through typing acts only: admission through the gate, a fork adopted, a pin changed. Not through market liquidity, not through prediction-market consensus, not through the urgency of an expiring position. The invariant already has mechanical guards in every deployment (the ranking-separation theorem's family); a bridge that finds a path from price to grade has found a bug, and the disclosure is worth more than the exploit.

**DON'T slash on judgment.** The field's hardest-won lesson, taken whole: penalties trigger on provable fault only, mechanically verifiable equivocation like attesting contradictory records, never on being wrong, unpopular, or early. Slashing on judgment is a political weapon within a week of deployment. What to slash, in what denomination, at what rate, is a community's parameter; that it triggers only on provable fault is the one fixed constraint the standing economy carries.

**DON'T build the singular bridge.** A single treasury, a single oracle, a single metering token for the ecosystem recreates the choke point this architecture exists to remove, and every safeguard the singular design then needs (sunset thresholds, successor registries, root-key abstinence) is the cost of the original mistake. Operators compete; treasuries are per-community or per-operator; tokens, where they exist at all, are one operator's metering instrument among many, and the protocol itself holds none, ever.

**DON'T promise what forks can't be forced to honor.** Cross-fork royalty continuity is a norm, not a mechanism: a fork that strips contributor distributions cannot be compelled, only seen, and being seen is real (the stripping is visible in the graph, exclusion-claimable, reputationally priced) but it is not enforcement. State it as the norm it is.

**DON'T let the market layer's failure reach the knowledge layer.** No kernel operation may depend on a bridge being solvent, honest, or alive. The dependency direction is one-way, the same arrow as periphery to kernel, and it is checkable: nothing in a kernel's suite may read a contract.

## The for-profit verifier, worked

The scenario that tests every rule above at once: a commercial kernel forks other kernels' claims wholesale, verifies them under its own staked attestation system, and sells the result to industry. Is that extraction? Run it through. Standing cannot be scraped: the crossing floors every imported claim, so grounded standing is remade with real local work or not obtained at all, and what arrives free is content plus unstrippable provenance. Enclosure is impossible, the source claims remain free everywhere; readers stay untolled, the customers buy warranties and liability, not access. If the verifier publishes its attestation graph, its verification work is itself free to read and crossable back into the sources, the parasite feeding the host by construction; if it stays private, it may, but its claims cannot cross into any public kernel without becoming public, and the commons has lost nothing it had.

What remains is free-riding on gifted content, and whether that gift had strings is the source community's declaration, which makes the **corpus content license a first-class community parameter**, carried in the parameter record and therefore inside the governance-hash, traveling visibly with every fork. The menu has teeth at one end: a gift license means the non-monetizing source is owed nothing and said so; attribution-required is already structural and becomes also legal; **share-alike is the commons' weapon**, requiring derivative verification layers to publish, which converts the commercial forker into an unpaid contributor of verification whether it likes the arrangement or not; non-commercial and named commercial terms route through the same accrual machinery as transcription royalties. Enforcement is legal, normative, and reputational, never mechanical, and the graph's role is visibility: a stripped license field or a one-way fork flow is there for anyone to see and price.

And the deepest correction to the scraping frame is where the creator's value actually lives: not in claim text but in the **attestation position**. The floor works for the creator, not only against the forker: a commercial verifier must re-earn every grade locally, and re-earning requires evidence, which means redoing the empirical work or obtaining the original attestations, and those belong to their makers. Fork any claim freely; the having-measured-it cannot be forked. Testimony, instrument chains, and data provenance are acts, acts are priceable, and they are priced by their holders, so every commercial re-grounding routes back through the primary evidence holders as a necessary input. Contributor migration toward paying kernels is then a labor market, not a collapse: the crossing is non-exclusive, so payers buy priority and warranty association, never enclosure, and the failure mode to actually watch is monopsony, against which the standing defense is plurality of kernels bidding for the same contributors.

Three residuals, stated rather than solved. Pure theorists and synthesists, whose work is genuinely text and structure with no instrument chain behind it, hold the weakest economic position: attribution-reputation, share-alike, and royalty treasuries are their channels, and this is the open-source maintainer problem inherited whole. The commons can lose curation energy to paying kernels even while keeping content access, the same problem's second face. And a payer can corrupt what it pays for, buying claim production though never standing elsewhere; the containment is visibility, bought content carries its community's governance-hash and its funding is attestable as a conflict-of-interest claim, quarantined and priceable downstream rather than prevented.

## Transcribed work, absent authors, and the commons

A transcription is a contribution like any other: claims typed, linked, and gated, with the citation machinery forcing the provenance bond to the source work, so attribution is structural at ingestion rather than optional afterward. A treasury's royalty policy can then split reference weight between the transcriber (whose structuring work is real and reference-counted normally) and the source's attribution targets, an ORCID, a DOI author record, a (name, work-hash) pair, at a ratio the community sets.

The authors' share follows the pattern **accrue openly, claim by proof**. Accrual requires nothing of the author: their balance accumulates against the attribution target whether or not they know the graph exists. Claiming requires one binding predicate at the grade the paying treasury demands: key K is bound to attribution target X, supported by a challenge-response against the external registry, an institutional proof, or co-author vouches. Not a maximal public identity; one strong binding, pseudonymous in every other respect if the author wishes, with the honest note that a payment address is itself linkage, so the fully anonymous claim waits on the selective-disclosure tier. Payout thresholds will sit high because money attached makes binding fraud provable-fault territory, and that is the parameter working as intended.

Absent authors and the intellectual commons share one pipe. Unclaimed balances escheat to the commons treasury after a policy window; public-domain and common-knowledge claims, whose attribution targets are diffuse or gone, route their reference weight to the commons at a floor rate from the start; and the royalty decay curve makes the whole thing continuous, rates decaying with age toward the commons floor rather than toward zero, so the oldest foundations fund the infrastructure everyone reads through. Scope stated plainly: none of this adjudicates copyright, and this document does not try to say where transcription sits in any jurisdiction's law of protected expression. What it builds is what the scraping decades never did: attribution that cannot be stripped, and a compensation hook that exists from the moment of ingestion, waiting for its author.

## The honest ledger

Open, characterized, not blocking anything above: the attestation oracle for genuinely off-graph facts (the field's decade of mechanism design converged on plural staked attestors and outlier rejection, and the problem remains the frontier); cross-fork royalty honoring as norm rather than mechanism; estates and legal succession for deceased authors' unclaimed balances, which is a legal-attestation question no graph mechanism settles; sybil-priced free access at the infrastructure margin (an institution masquerading as ten thousand individuals is a metering problem for operators, not a graph problem, and current answers are heuristic); regulatory classification of any operator's metering token, jurisdiction-dependent and outside the protocol's power to fix; and settlement latency for markets on slow-verifying claims, where partial settlement at intermediate grades is possible precisely because the grade lattice is ordered, at the cost of settling against a moving fact, a market-design choice, not a protocol one.

The summary is the separation held all the way down: contributors are paid from usage by treasuries anyone can audit against the graph; speculators earn from markets that reference grades and can never move them; institutions pay operators for convenience over a commons nobody can sell; individuals read free not by subsidy but by construction; and the only singular thing anywhere is the graph itself, which is singular the way arithmetic is singular, the same for everyone because anyone can recompute it.
