# Epistemic Uplift

Epistemic uplift is the competition's first criterion, and the honest question is not whether this structure helps on the three cases, which the guided path shows, but where the help comes from and whether it generalizes. It comes from a single equation. Uplift is trust composed with view: the kernel establishes trust once, computed and checkable, and a periphery supplies a view fitted to one reader, and the uplift is the composition of the two. A market is the space of all such compositions over one kernel.

The two degenerate cases say what that means. The kernel alone is trust with no view, inert. Synthesis is view with no trust, which is why it neither compounds nor supports a market: synthesis fuses trust and view into one artifact, the author who grounds is the author who presents, inseparably, so the grounding cannot be reused without the author and no second view can rest on the same trust because the trust does not exist apart from the prose that carries it. The kernel's one move is to separate trust from view. That single cut is the whole submission seen from one angle. The critique of synthesis is that it fuses them. The kernel is the thing that splits them. Compounding is what the split enables on the trust side, and a market is what it enables on the view side. Two payoffs of one cut.

## A market of views over shared trust

A synthesis supports one interface: reading it. Every question a reader might ask, what is load-bearing here, where does this turn, what happens if this assumption fails, is answered by re-reading and re-reasoning, because there is no object underneath to query and no trust apart from the prose. A typed graph with mechanical grounding is the opposite. Grounding is a computation over structure, so an interface is a view over that structure, and many views compose over one kernel without any of them re-deriving what grounds. The point is not that one could build a better single view. The point is that a checkable substrate lets many parties build competing, specialized views over the same knowledge, and a reader chooses among them without re-establishing the trust each time.

What such a market contains is illustrated by the lenses this submission ships thin, each a different question asked of the same structure. A load-bearing view walks the support cone and marks the claims whose removal drops a conclusion's grade, computing what a synthesis can only assert. An assumption-flip view flips a claim and shows the downstream grades give way, so a reader sees the shape of an argument's dependence rather than being told it, which is the LHC framework swap and the eggs denominator swap as a control anyone can operate. A crux view shows the frontier where two contradicting conclusions' support cones diverge, repricing a stuck debate as a disagreement over one node held in the open, which is the COVID prior surfaced. A provenance view walks a claim's history back through its crossings and forks to its origin, so a reader can see where a claim grounded and who retyped it rather than trusting a citation. A gap view lists what is unmeasured, each claim whose kind could ground higher than it does, with the measurement that would close it, which is the regenerative soil-carbon leaps read as leaps. A dependence view shows correlated support treated as independent, the shared node under lines that look separate.

These are examples, not a menu. In a market, a crux specialist and a provenance walker and a plain-language explainer are different parties building rival versions of each lens, and none of them has to be trusted about the grounding, because the kernel settled that and none of them can touch it. So the market competes on how to show the knowledge while agreeing on the knowledge, which is the inversion that matters: a market of information usually fragments truth, everyone arriving with their own facts, and this one cannot, because trust was separated out and made shared, so there is nothing left for the market to fragment. It is healthy specialization with none of the usual cost.

That reframes the reader, and it is the actual uplift. In a market of views over one trust, the reader does not receive a view, the reader composes one, moving from the explainer to the crux specialist to the gap view over the same claims, and the trust holds across every move because it was established once and no tool re-litigates it. Single-tool uplift has a ceiling, because a tool serves the reader it was built for. Market uplift does not, because a new periphery specializes for whoever the incumbents serve badly, and it enters over the contract without touching the core. That is the competition's scaling question, a diverse and changing user base and an expanding frontier of tooling, answered the only way it scales, by an ecosystem rather than a monolith. The design principle underneath is singularize trust, pluralize view: trust must be one thing or it fragments, views must be many or they do not serve diverse readers, and an agnostic core with a swappable periphery is exactly that, singular where singularity matters and plural where it does not.

Reader freedom is graduated, and naming the two markets keeps it honest. Within one kernel there is a market of peripheries, competing views over shared trust, and a reader moves among them freely. Across kernels there is a market of kernels, competing trust bases joined by the untyped bridges, which is the federation, and a reader moves among those at the untyped-crossing cost. Free within a trust base, costed across trust bases, which is top-down inside a kernel and bottom-up across them, the taxonomy seen from the reader's side.

The market is enabled, not guaranteed, and the mechanism that enables it is the external contract. The three thin peripheries in the tree are a market of one author. The external contract is what lets peripheries built by others read grounded claims and propose new ones over the kernel, which is what turns the uplift from single-author to an actual market, so the external contract is not a side capability but the mechanism that makes the market claim real. Whether a market forms is an adoption question, the same honesty as minimal periphery: the architecture makes a market possible and the contract lowers the barrier to entry, and the claim is that the structure unlocks a market, not that one exists. And the market amplifies the kernel in both directions, since many good views over bad grounding is many views of garbage, but the grounding is checkable and a bad kernel is forkable, so the market is self-correcting through the fork rather than by trusting the kernel to be good.

## Teaching is downstream of claim

The competition's first criterion benchmarks uplift against off-the-shelf research and coding assistants, which deliver teaching uplift: they bring a reader up to speed, explain a topic, make an investigator faster. That is real and those tools do it well. The knowledge itself is a second thing, whether the claim as an object is checkable, composable, and able to stand when trust in its asserter is subtracted, and this is claim uplift. The two are not orthogonal. They are ordered, and the order is the trust-view cut seen once more.

One direction is empty. A better synthesis teaches better and leaves the claim beneath it exactly as checkable as it was, which is not at all, because a synthesis fuses trust and view and a clearer fusion is still a fusion. There is no separable object that a better explanation made verifiable; extracting the claim into something a second party can check is a distinct act, performed by whoever builds the kernel, and the synthesis confers none of it. Teaching does not reach claim.

The other direction carries, on two channels. The structure teaches the shape of a dispute, what is load-bearing, where the crux sits, what is measured and what is declared, what is settled and what merely performs settling, and it teaches exactly what a fluent synthesis buries, because a synthesis smooths the counterexample flat while the structure reifies it as a node a reader can inspect. This is not teaching a topic faster, which the structure does not especially do; it is teaching a thing a synthesis has no object to teach, and the guided path is where a reader meets it, walking the LHC reprice or the COVID crux and learning something true about the dispute that no synthesis taught. The market is the second channel: every view over the kernel is a teacher, an unbounded number stand over one trust base, and none re-charges the trust, whereas a synthesis that teaches well is its trust, fused, so it admits exactly one teacher. The trust base carries more teaching than any single synthesis can, and carries it because the trust was separated out.

One mechanism runs under both channels. Teaching is a view, presentation fitted to a reader whose purpose is to understand, and a view stands on a trust-side object. A synthesis holds no such objects apart from its prose, so there is nothing for a second teaching-view to stand on and the reverse direction stays empty; the kernel makes the crux, the load-bearing node, the frame-dependence into objects, and once they are objects a view can teach them. Claim uplift teaches by creating the objects teaching stands on. The ordering is guaranteed rather than contingent because view forms over trust and trust does not need view, and teaching over claim is that same cut applied to uplift. The direct channel is modest and present, the market channel is enabled and not guaranteed, and both sit downstream of the one axis this submission builds.

## The exemplary efforts this structure would sharpen

The composition equation has two halves, and the section above is the view half, what a periphery contributes. This is the trust half, what the kernel contributes, argued on the competition's own list. The competition names a collection of strong epistemic work, and read as a list it is a catalog of capabilities the kernel provides as structure, each entry someone supplying by discipline what the structure would supply by construction.

Transparent Replications catalogs importance-hacking by separating what was measured from how it was framed. The kernel separates them by type: a claim grounds to its floor, and its framing is a distinct forum node the claim depends on by presupposition, so the gap between what a result measures and what it is presented as claiming is a structural fact, not a judgment call reached case by case.

Elisabeth Bik's forensic image analysis exposes threats to integrity, and its value compounds only if a finding sticks and travels. The exclusion record is where a refuted claim stays with the reason it fell, so a forensic finding is not re-litigated: a dead claim meets its own obituary on any attempt at revival, and the forensic work accretes rather than being redone.

Nadel and Pritchett's construct-validity critique is that the same program is a different thing across sites. That is exactly the population-mismatch discriminator the atlas already holds: a statistic supports a conclusion only if the sample is representative of the target, and a violation at that precondition is a typed departure, so the critique the field kept papering over is a structural check rather than a periodic reminder.

Flake and Fried's measurement scrutiny exposes questionable measurement practices, claims dressed as measurements that do not measure what they claim. The floor typing is that dressing: a claim that asserts a measurement floor without the record that reaches it does not ground there, so a questionable measurement fails to earn the standing its presentation asks for, by the same rule for everyone.

Data Colada's investigations turn on the veritability and origin of datasets. That is provenance and claim history: a claim carries the chain back to its source, and a dataset's standing is a traceable object rather than an assumed one, so the question Data Colada asks by hand, where did this actually come from and does it hold, is a walk the structure supports.

Leveson's systems-safety work keeps encountering specifications out of date as soon as published, and builds methods for contexts that change under you. That is the living structure with forkability and standards-from-use: the graph updates as claims change and as new sources land, a stale citation is a checkable fact, and the schema itself grows from accumulated forks rather than ossifying at publication, which is the mindset her work asks for made mechanical.

Examine.com grades nutrition evidence claim-by-claim, parsing study quality and effect sizes and updating as new trials land. That is almost a description of the kernel: grounding is claim-by-claim, the grade is a property of what a claim rests on, and decay and supersession are how a standing changes when a new trial lands. The egg case is this done by the structure rather than by a standing editorial effort.

Andrew Gelman's blog is a running catalog of overconfident conclusions and shaky extrapolations. The contamination rule is the catch: a claim is as grounded as its weakest necessary support and no more, so an overconfident conclusion cannot advertise a standing its evidence does not carry, and the shaky extrapolation is caught at the join rather than in a post-hoc critique.

The Society Library's Diablo Canyon investigation engages the whole range of perspectives on a complex topic with deep chains of reasoning across subtopics. That is composition and the forum: many perspectives held as structure, their weighings visible and unsettled, and deep reasoning chains carried across subtopics by the same grounding rule, which is what lets a genuinely multi-perspective investigation compound instead of collapsing to one narrative.

Heuer's analysis of competing hypotheses is structured reasoning under adversarial, information-scarce conditions. That is reconciliation with a computed crux: competing hypotheses held against one another, the evidence they share in the resolved region, and the frontier where they diverge computed rather than argued, which is the discipline Heuer championed given a mechanism.

Read together, the list is the trust half of the equation proven on the field's own examples: ten efforts recognized as strong, each supplying by discipline a capability the kernel supplies by structure. Composed with the view half, a market of interfaces over that trust, the two halves are the whole claim. Uplift is trust established once and checkable, composed with a view fitted to a reader, and a market of such views over one kernel is what makes the composition beat any monolith, because the thing each strong effort does carefully once becomes a property of the graph that holds for everyone, accretes, and can be shown through as many lenses as the market builds.
