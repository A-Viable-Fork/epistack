# The Coordination Layer

The kernel computes a claim's grade from its supports, taking the weakest necessary support and letting convergence add with diminishing returns, so no author assigns a grade and the structure decides it. That computation is built and checked. The coordination layer is the same computation extended from claims to the people who make them: a participant's standing is a reading of their record the way a grade is a reading of a claim's support, computed and current, never assigned and never held. This document specifies that extension. The grade machinery it builds on runs today; the coordination layer it describes is the specified frontier, and this draws the line between them sharply, because the layer's credibility is that it grows from something that works rather than being invented beside it.

The frontier is where the kernel stops being one author's instrument and becomes many communities validating and building on each other's claims. That needs three things the single-author build never required: a way for scrutiny to happen without an authority assigning it, a way for a participant's weight to track their record without becoming a currency, and a way for standing to mean something when a claim crosses between communities that run different rules. Each of these is the kernel's own principles applied one level up, and each is specified here at the level of the function it must perform, leaving the specific form free, because the shape a function must have to keep authority distributed is defensible now while the best form to give it is something the ecosystem discovers.

## The spine: standing is to participants what grade is to claims

A grade is a computed reading. The gate takes a claim's supports, finds the weakest necessary one, adds convergence with diminishing returns, and reads off a grade. Nobody grants it. A claim cited by one source sits where one source puts it; a second independent source lifts it less than the first did; and the reading is always current because it is recomputed from the supports, never stored as a verdict. That is the move the whole kernel turns on, and it is built.

Standing extends it. A participant's standing is the same kind of reading, computed from what they have authored that survived scrutiny and what scrutiny they have done that held up, current, and diminishing in its returns to tenure the way a grade diminishes in its returns to convergence. Standing is never granted by an authority and never held as a balance, because a reading cannot be granted or held, only read. Everything that follows is what it takes to make that reading resist the attacks a many-participant system invites, and every defense is a property the reading must have rather than a rule an authority enforces.

## The vocabulary the layer is written in: function, contract, standard

The layer is written in three layers. A function is a slot and the job it does, stated as the abstract job and never as a way of doing it: gaming-resistant validation, Sybil-resistant weighting, self-scaling scrutiny. A function is a classification, the set of contracts that do this job, and it is what must match for two communities to coordinate, because it is the shared meaning their standing translates across. A function names no mechanism.

A contract is a specific mechanism that does the job, identified by its type and hash: a random draw, an adversarial-selection rule, a log curve, a supply-side toll. A contract has a form, its type, the specific mechanism chosen, and parameters, its values, each set flat or set to float on the community's own local state, the form the part that does not float and the parameters the part that can. Contracts are compared by hash, not checked against the function by any authority, and a contract's fitness for its function is revealed in use and selected by adoption, the forms-market, rather than certified up front. Whether a given contract belongs to a function, that this random draw is gaming-resistant, is itself a claim, grounded and contestable the way any claim in the system is grounded, not ruled on by a certifier, so the coordination layer validates its own contracts the way the kernel validates claims.

A standard is a body's declaration of which contracts it accepts for which function, ranging from permissive, any contract of this function, to restrictive, one named contract-hash. A standard is itself hashable and forkable, set per body at whatever level governs: a bottom-up federation may require only that each function be filled by sufficient contracts, an organization may require specific contract classes, a government-like body may mandate one exact contract. A standard that binds by class references the function, and whether a given contract is in that class is the grounded, challengeable claim above, not a gate an authority runs. A body's standard is authoritative within it and escapable by forking the body, which is how a standard can be prescriptive without being a permanent center.

Two communities coordinate when their standards are compatible, and standing composes when the contracts a claim passed through meet the receiving standard, checked by hash-comparison rather than by an authority. This is the same fixed-shared-free line the parameters register draws for the kernel, now at three levels: the function is the shared job that must match, the contract is the free local mechanism, and the standard is the per-body binding over which contracts fill which functions.

## The validation economy: scrutiny without an assigner

A many-participant commons has to get claims scrutinized, and the naive way, an authority assigning validators, is the center the whole architecture removes. The function is self-scaling scrutiny: the supply of validation must scale with the supply of claims without an authority assigning it. One contract that satisfies it is a supply-side toll, where writing any claim requires validation work on others' claims, so the participant most able to flood low-quality claims is the one who must do the most validating to do it. A community's contract sets how much work a write costs, flat or floating on the queue's depth so a backed-up queue prices its own relief.

The function here is gaming-resistant validation: every claim must be covered by opposed scrutiny, so that lazy or captured validation exposes itself. Gaming the validation is a non-move in the same plain sense the gate makes gaming one: the admissible moves are the ones the opposed roles recognize, and a lazy or colluding validation does not slip through as a move, it exposes itself to its opposite. What the function requires is the opposition, a verifier trying to confirm the support holds and a red-teamer trying to break it, because a lazy verifier and a lazy red-teamer cannot both pass: if the claim is flawed the honest red-teamer's catch exposes the lazy verifier, and if it is sound the honest verifier exposes the manufactured objection. How the opposed roles are staffed is a contract. One contract is random assignment, which defeats collusion by making the target unsteerable, so no validator can choose whose claim they cover. Another is adversarial selection, which assigns the most opposed participant to red-team and the most supportive to verify, defeating collusion by motivated opposition rather than by unpredictability. Both satisfy the function differently, and a community picks the contract and sets how many validators beyond the opposed pair, flat or floating on the claim's stakes. What holds across the choice is the function, adversarial coverage on every claim; the staffing rule is the contract that fills it.

What a validator adds is precisely the part the gate cannot compute. The gate checks a claim's structure, whether its supports carry the grade it declares. A validator checks the claim's fidelity to the world, whether a cited source says what it is claimed to say, whether an independent corroboration is genuinely independent. So the forum does not second-guess the gate, it does the job the gate cannot, which is the gated write the frontier always named, now with a mechanism. And because a validation is itself a claim, that I checked this support and it holds, it is tolled like any claim, by validation work that shrinks at each level so the recursion terminates rather than running forever. The economy starts from a seed corpus that did not pay the toll, the cases authored before the forum existed, named here as the genesis rather than pretended to be self-starting.

## Standing: a weighting, not a currency

Standing has one job: to say whose validation counts for how much, so that a decade-long record's judgment outweighs a fresh identity's. It is earned, computed from the record, which is fixed because it is the denomination the whole economy reads in. It is a weighting and never a currency, which is the fixed choice that does the most work in the layer: because standing is only ever a weight on how much you count and is never spent, transferred, or held, it cannot be accumulated and dumped at a strategic moment, cannot be bought or sold into a reputation market, and even a stake is only a temporary lowering of your own weight as a bond rather than a token risked. Timing games have nothing to time and the commons has nothing to trade, both gone because standing is kept a reading rather than a holding, the same way a grade is a reading rather than a holding.

Standing weights newcomers below established participants, which is the Sybil defense. The function is Sybil-resistant weighting, and it is stated as a property any weighting contract must have: concave so its returns to tenure diminish, threshold-free so a newcomer is quiet rather than locked out, with a small nonzero weight from the first day that grows fastest early and levels late. A step function would lock newcomers out and build a gerontocracy; a convex function would keep them near-weightless for so long it amounts to the same; a threshold would create a cliff an attacker's parallel identities cross together on the same day. So concavity and threshold-freeness are the function stated as a required property, and they turn the openness-versus-security knife-edge into the shape of a curve rather than the placement of a number. A log is one contract that has this shape, welcoming at the bottom and flattening at the top; a root is another; which is best no one knows, and the community picks the contract and sets its steepness, flat or floating so ascent slows automatically when the system reads a Sybil-like pattern in its own participation. The input the curve reads is active tenure, so that inactivity both stops accrual and, through a decay contract, bleeds standing away, which satisfies a further function, sleeper-resistance, defending against the participant who ages a dormant identity and activates it for a strike.

That single concave curve over active tenure can govern both how much a participant's validation counts and how fast their claims take effect, so the defense against parallel-Sybil and the defense against bursts are one contract, which is the clean default. A community whose attack profile needs the two tuned apart may decompose them into separate delays instead. Whether it is one contract or several is free; that the weighting is concave and threshold-free, that standing decays with inactivity, and that a burst cannot take instant effect are the fixed properties underneath either choice.

## The telos: where a kernel's purpose lives

Every other mechanism is purpose-neutral. The gate does not care what you investigate; the crossing does not care what domain. The telos is the one place a kernel says what it is for: a priority ordering over the validation queue, so a safety-critical kernel can let scrutiny reach safety claims first and a general commons can hold everything even. It is a function slot filled by a contract, flat by default, and a general commons leaves it flat, which is itself a statement, that it pushes no thesis and treats all claims alike.

The telos is also the one place soft capture could enter, because an author who weighted the queue toward the claims that flatter a thesis would be steering standing toward preferred conclusions without ever touching a grade. So the fixed property on the telos is that it is declared, inspectable, and forkable: visible so no weighting is hidden, forkable so a community that dislikes an ordering leaves for one it sets itself. A telos you can see and leave is a purpose the community chose; a telos hidden or unforkable is a hand on the scale. The layer keeps the telos narrow, a priority over the queue and no more, and resists letting it grow into a general ranking engine, because the narrow version is honest orientation and the broad version is the attention-steering machine that the worst of every platform grows from.

## The three-way parameter taxonomy

The layer sorts three ways, and the sort is its structural claim. The fixed tier is the smallest and most abstract: it is the functions themselves, the jobs that must be done, gaming-resistant validation, self-scaling scrutiny, Sybil-resistant weighting, sleeper-resistance, a purpose the community can see and leave, standing earned as a weighting and never a currency, and where a function is stated as a property any contract must have, that property, concavity and threshold-freeness the clearest case. The fixed tier names no mechanism, and it never varies and never floats, because each function keeps authority distributed by keeping standing computed rather than granted, and a guarantee that varied under load would open a hole exactly when it is needed. Below it is contract: the mechanisms that fill the functions, an opposed-draw or adversarial-selection rule, a supply-side toll, a concave curve, a decay function, a telos ordering, each identified by hash and each with a form and its parameters. Over the contracts sit the standards, a body's per-function binding on which contracts it accepts. A contract's parameters split in turn: the free-static tier is what a community sets and leaves, a flat toll, a chosen curve steepness, a decay rate, a telos ordering; the free-floating tier is what a community sets as a function of its own local state, a toll that floats on queue depth, an ascent that floats on participation-health, each a governor that regulates throughput without anyone setting a number.

The floating tier is powerful and it is where the layer is least certain, so the honesty is explicit: a floating parameter is a control loop, and a control loop can oscillate rather than settle, its stability set by the shape of the function and how fast it responds. A community floating several parameters is running a multi-loop system whose joint stability is not guaranteed by each loop being stable alone. And a floating parameter must float on local state only, never on federation-global state, because governors coupled across the whole federation would couple every community's economy into one loop no one controls, a soft center entering through the dynamics. Float on local load, and each community stays sovereign in its economy as in everything else.

## Federation: the governance-hash

A community's full parameterization, the contracts filling every function, is a content-addressed bundle with a governance-hash, the same content-addressing that freezes a type's meaning so types compose across kernels. The three layers each have their hash and their check: the governance-hash certifies the contracts a community runs; a standard certifies which contracts a body accepts for which function; and composition checks that the contract-hashes a claim passed through meet the receiving standard, by hash-comparison, not by an authority. A claim carries the governance-hash it earned its standing under, so standing composes across communities because what it meant is frozen and inspectable. Governances and standards become comparable as data, which gives the choice of contracts an empirical substrate: the best contract for a function is found by studying the hashes of communities that thrived, not decreed here, so the contracts are themselves a market and this specification names them rather than settling them.

The relation between two standards is always computable, and the translation of standing across it follows the typing relation the vocabulary set. Where the standards are ordered, one strictly stricter on the relevant functions, standing flows down-hill into a looser standard freely and up-hill into a stricter one only capped: it does not translate up until re-earned under contracts the stricter standard accepts, held until then. The up-hill cap is the untyped-type move a third time: cheap standing arrives capped and grounding little, until re-earned under accepted contracts, exactly as an unadopted type grounds nothing until an owned fork types it, and free up-hill translation would otherwise make the loosest standard a laundering door into the whole federation. Where two standards are unordered, sharing a function but accepting different contracts for it, the translation does not compute itself; the system surfaces the difference and asks the receiving community for an explicit translation choice, which is itself hashable and adoptable, so translations across unordered boundaries accumulate as their own market. A shared contract-hash composes automatically, a different contract for the same function needs a choice, and a function the receiving standard does not require at all is the hard incompatibility, the automation gradient reading straight off the hashes.

Changing a governance produces a new hash, and old claims stay pinned to the hash they were validated under, so change is never retroactive and staleness against a community's current rules is a visible checkable fact through the same versioned-pointer machinery that reports type staleness. The hash describes and composes without compelling, because every governance is forkable and every claim can cross to different rules, so no hash is authoritative. Governance change is itself a validated act under a community's own rules, and the backstop when a community changes its rules in a way a participant rejects is the backstop everywhere: fork at the last governance-hash you trusted, and let adoption decide which fork lives.

## What is built and what is specified

The grade computation this layer extends is built and checked: the weakest-support reading, the diminishing returns of convergence, the untyped type that grounds nothing until forked, the content-addressing that freezes meaning across a crossing, and the forkability that is the backstop under all of it. The coordination layer is specified, not built. Its mechanisms are designed here to the level of the function each must perform and the fixed properties each must hold, with illustrative forms shown as fillings rather than answers, and its magnitudes left to the communities that would run it. It is the frontier the status ledger marks specified, developed far enough that a builder could start and a reader can see it grows from the kernel's own grade computation rather than standing apart from it.

## Open edges, the frontier's own frontier

The joint stability of several floating parameters is not guaranteed and is a property to be tested per configuration, not claimed. Which form best fills each slot is unknown and is left to the market of forms the governance-hashes make analyzable. The steepness of the concave weighting is a real openness-against-security tradeoff a community must set with no universally right value. The economy bootstraps from a seed corpus named honestly as its genesis. And tradable stake is the line past which standing becomes a currency and the commons becomes a market; the layer stays on the earned, non-tradable side of it and names the crossing as beyond its scope. These are stated plainly because a specification of a frontier earns belief the way the rest of the submission does, by being exact about what it has and what it has not.
