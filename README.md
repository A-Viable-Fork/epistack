# EpiStack

Knowledge is the part of a claim that stands when trust in its asserter is taken away. EpiStack is the engine that computes that part.

Begin by subtraction. An authoritative tone once vouched for a claim; machines now produce it by the page, so set it aside. A wide bibliography once vouched for a claim; machines produce that too, formatted, plausible, and unread, so set it aside. A known name once vouched for a claim; names now sign text they never examined, so set the name aside. Each signal held its force while producing it cost something. The cost has fallen to nothing, and the force fell with it.

What remains after the subtraction is the floor: whatever a claim rests on that a reader can check while trusting no one. A measurement repeats. A proof verifies. A declaration is a public act whose issuance anyone can confirm. Some claims reach a floor. Others rest on argument, and an honest engine holds those in the open, in a forum, their weighings visible and marked unsettled.

The floor composes. A question inside one domain grounds to that domain's floor. A question spanning domains composes their floors and carries its weighings in the open, where a reader can reprice them.

EpiStack builds up from there. It stores claims as a typed graph with the support between them. Each claim grounds to a floor, a measurement, a proof, or a declaration, or sits in the forum when argument is what it rests on. A new claim enters through a gate: it is admitted when it holds together with what is already there. A claim's standing is a property of the graph, propagated by one rule, a claim is as grounded as its weakest necessary support and contested support carries its contest forward, so any reader can recompute any claim's standing from scratch. Three worked cases run on the engine: LHC safety, a confident answer over complex evidence; COVID origins, a curated debate; egg nutrition, a mundane contested question. One machinery carries each case to its own termination.
