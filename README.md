# EpiStack

EpiStack is a top-down meta kernel: one designed schema for claims and their grounding, demonstrably spanning three domains that share nothing, physics measurement, epidemiological contestation, normative weighing. To see why that sentence is the contribution, begin by clearing synthesis.

Synthesis is what the strongest tools do, and they do it well. A good synthesis reads cleanly, integrates across sources, surfaces connections a reader would have missed, and the machines do all of this better each month. Concede the strength in full; the clearing is credible only if the strength is real. Every synthesis is organized for a reader who supplies the trust. Its structure lives in prose, so the reader receives sentences and a request for belief, and checking any step means reconstructing the whole from the sources. A synthesis stands exactly as long as its reader's trust does, and under challenge it dissolves back into its bibliography. Cheap generation finishes the clearing: when a plausible synthesis costs nothing to produce, the trust it asks for is the entire product, and that trust now hangs on air.

Knowledge is what stands when trust in the knower is removed, and it is made to stand by replacing that trust with a warrant that does not need the knower. Where a claim can be checked, the warrant is verification: a valid proof asks you to trust nothing about the prover, only to check the steps, so one proof plus its review removes the prover in a single move. Where a claim cannot be checked in one instance, the warrant is convergence: one physicist's measurement is testimony you either trust or do not, but many independent measurements compose into knowledge that holds when no single measurer is load-bearing. One measurement is testimony; many are knowledge. A proof is knowledge once checked. Both are the same move, trust in the knower replaced by something that does not need the knower, and a synthesis can make neither move, because its synthesizer is never verified and never subtracted, only trusted. The structure makes both warrants properties of a graph: the gate verifies what can be checked, and composition subtracts the knower from the rest.

The kernel is the machinery that grounds claims to floors: a typed graph, a gate that admits only contributions holding together with what is already there, and one propagation rule, a claim is as grounded as its weakest necessary support, contested support carries its contest forward, and any reader recomputes any claim's standing from scratch while trusting no one. Watch it work and notice what it never knows: the kernel has no idea what a claim is about, nor what produced it. Floors are domain-specific, a measurement in physics, an estimate in epidemiology, a declaration for a definition; the kernel treats any floor uniformly. It is also producer-agnostic: it checks a claim's structure, not the claimant's nature, so a producer, meaning anything that emits claims, human or model, is checked the same. Two axes have traveled under one word, trust and domain, and pulling them apart is the design. There is one kernel in the trust sense, and it is domain-agnostic by construction.

An agnostic core admits more than one arrangement of domains above it, and the arrangements sit on one axis, the direction of authority over the schema. A domain-specific kernel covers one subject with its own schema and floors, the base unit. A bottom-up meta kernel federates independent domain kernels that keep their own schemas, joined at a shared untyped type, authority staying with the members. A top-down meta kernel starts from one schema spanning the domains as regions within it, authority in the schema, composition native. The untyped type is the hinge that makes this an axis: one type every schema shares, holding foreign imports and locally untyped claims alike, grounding nothing, so standing crosses only when an author forks it across, retail per claim or wholesale per category, and retail forks accumulating into wholesale ones is how shared vocabulary grows from use. Each end of the axis has its regime: top-down buys coherence and native composition at the price of one schema, right for a single author spanning a unified analysis; bottom-up buys federation and incremental adoption at the price of lossy crossings, right for many authors who will never agree on types.

EpiStack is the top-down point on that axis, built for its regime, and the three cases are the proof it does real epistemic work. Each yields the counterexample a good synthesis would have smoothed flat. The LHC safety argument looks robust, several lines converging, until the graph reifies the dependency the lines share as a node and the convergence reprices as one assumption wearing several coats. The COVID origins evidence looks decisive, in whichever direction the teller favors, until the prior the verdict turns on is named as a claim of its own and the debate resolves into one weighing, held in the open where a reader can reprice it. The egg nutrition verdict looks like a fact until the frame it presupposes, which population, which endpoint, which food it replaces, is swapped and the verdict swaps with it. Three domains, three floors, three counterexamples, one schema that never knew the difference.

The periphery around the kernel is thin by choice, the smallest surface that shows what a checkable core enables, reading through one contract so richer tools swap in as models improve. And the schema is a base others can fork: the untyped type already sits at the bottom of the ordering, which is the hinge a federation of many authors will need. The top-down kernel is what was built; the bottom-up federation is what it points to.
