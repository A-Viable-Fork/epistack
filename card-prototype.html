a step in the reasoning

Can you trust your sample?

Where the cases turn up can only tell you where something started if you counted them fairly everywhere. Look harder in some places than others, and your map shows where you looked, not where things are.

The one line of math

The cases you see at a place equals the cases really there, times how hard you looked there.

how many cases you counted at place x
how many cases are really at place x
how thoroughly you searched place x

This only works if you searched everywhere equally, so that detect(x) is the same number at every place. Then what you see is a faithful copy of what is there.

See it

Cases are spread evenly across the area. Sweep your searchlight and watch what you see.

The field underneath never changes. Only your searchlight moves.

Where it breaks

Suppose early testing happened mostly around one market. You find a cluster of cases there, not because it started there, but because that is where you looked. The cluster is a picture of your searchlight, not the source.

Why it matters

Every conclusion that reads meaning from where the cases are, origin, spread, the hottest spot, depends on this step holding. If it fails, those conclusions are reading the searchlight. This is the step the COVID origin argument turns on.
